## Strategic Analysis

### Why Roller Disco Economics Are Challenging

1. **Safety costs are non-negotiable:**
   - Insurance with skating coverage: $1,500-2,000
   - On-site EMT: $480-720
   - Mandatory waivers, safety protocols
   - **Total safety overhead:** $2-2.5K (applies to ALL scenarios)

2. **Venue + skate infrastructure is expensive:**
   - **Option A (Built-in rink):** Church of 8 Wheels at $8K
   - **Option B (Custom build):** Warehouse $5K + flooring $8K = $13K
   - **Option C (Suburban rink):** Skate Escape $5K but location penalty

3. **Production needs are high:**
   - Skating requires big open floor = large lighting/sound setup
   - Disco aesthetic demands disco balls, blacklights, neon (not cheap)
   - Active environment needs robust sound system (louder than standing events)

4. **Sponsor acquisition is uncertain:**
   - Skate brands (Moxi, Impala) are niche, may not have $8-12K event budgets
   - Athletic brands (Nike, Adidas) typically sponsor larger events (500+ guests)
   - Energy drinks (Red Bull) possible but competitive (many event requests)

5. **Ticket price ceiling is real:**
   - Market research: $45-65 is typical for SF activity events
   - Roller skating is perceived as "fun/casual" not "premium/exclusive"
   - Hard to justify $75+ ticket when bowling/arcade bars are $30-40

## Risk Assessment

### Financial Risks
1. **Ticket sales shortfall:**
   - **Risk:** Only sell 150 tickets instead of 250 → loss increases to -$8K+
   - **Mitigation:** Pre-sale validation, aggressive marketing 8 weeks out
   - **Backup:** Cancel event if <150 sold by 3 weeks before event

2. **Sponsor acquisition failure:**
   - **Risk:** Only secure $6K sponsors (vs $12K target) → $6K additional loss
   - **Mitigation:** Start sponsor outreach 10 weeks early, multiple brand targets
   - **Backup:** Increase ticket to $75-85 if sponsors fall short

3. **Venue unavailability:**
   - **Risk:** Church of 8 Wheels fully booked or won't discount below $8K
   - **Mitigation:** Have 3 backup venues researched simultaneously
   - **Backup:** Consider "Skate & Sip" hybrid model at bar venue

### Operational Risks
1. **Injuries/liability:**
   - **Risk:** Guest injury during skating → lawsuit, reputation damage
   - **Mitigation:** $2M liability insurance, mandatory waivers, on-site EMT, safety briefings
   - **Backup:** Have legal counsel review all waiver documents pre-event

2. **Alcohol + skating combination:**
   - **Risk:** Intoxicated guests on skates = injury magnet
   - **Mitigation:** Strict wristband system, bar cutoff at 9 PM (skating until 10 PM), security monitoring
   - **Backup:** "Skate first, drink after" event flow (skating 7-9 PM, bar 9-11 PM)

3. **Low attendance due to skill intimidation:**
   - **Risk:** People afraid they can't skate well = stay home
   - **Mitigation:** Marketing emphasizes "all skill levels," roaming instructors, "slow skate" periods
   - **Backup:** Offer "non-skater" tickets at $40 (bar access only, no skates)
