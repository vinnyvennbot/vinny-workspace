## Strategic Recommendations

### ✅ PURSUE: Scenario B (Flora Grubb Gardens)
1. **Lock Flora Grubb Gardens** early - popular event space, books out quickly
   - Working nursery provides built-in botanical backdrop (saves decor costs)
   - Outdoor setting = spacious for 3 bar stations
   - Quirky SF charm aligns with Venn's brand (unique > generic)

2. **Secure $5K gin distillery partnership:**
   - **Primary target: Distillery 209** (San Francisco-based craft gin)
     - Botanical ingredients align perfectly (juniper, citrus, herbs)
     - Local brand wants SF event visibility
     - Can provide product + mixology expertise
   - **Backup: St. George Spirits** (Alameda-based, gin + liqueurs)
   - **Offer:**
     - Exclusive spirits provider (all cocktails feature their gin)
     - Branded bar stations, cocktail recipe cards
     - Product education component (botanical ingredients tasting)
     - Social media content, influencer partnerships
     - 4 VIP tickets for brand reps/ambassadors

3. **Optimize mixology costs:**
   - Hire 3 mixologists at $150/hr (vs. $175+ at agencies)
   - Source through bartender referrals vs. agency markup
   - Partner with SF Bartenders Guild for talent pool

4. **Tiered ticket pricing strategy:**
   - **Early bird:** $70 (first 40 tickets) - drives early momentum
   - **Regular:** $75 (next 50 tickets) - standard pricing
   - **Last call:** $85 (final 10 tickets) - scarcity urgency
   - **Average:** $74/person - achieves break-even with sponsor

5. **Catering optimization:**
   - Partner with local bakery for botanical desserts ($12-15/person)
   - Plus light passed appetizers ($10-12/person = $22-27 total)
   - Focus on Instagram-worthy presentation (edible flowers, herb garnishes)
   - Target $25/person all-in (vs. $28-35 at fancy caterers)

6. **Venue-specific perks:**
   - Negotiate takeaway plants at cost through Flora Grubb ($3/plant vs. $5 retail)
   - Use venue's existing outdoor lighting (reduce production costs)
   - Built-in garden aesthetic = minimal decor needed

**With these optimizations:**
- Revenue: $7,400 (tickets) + $5,000 (sponsor) = $12,400
- Expenses: $12,000 (optimized)
- **Net: +$400 (break-even, small profit buffer)** ✅

## Risk Mitigation

### Financial Risks
1. **Ticket sales shortfall:**
   - **Mitigation:** Early bird pricing creates urgency, target SF cocktail enthusiasts via IG ads
   - **Backup:** Reduce capacity to 80 guests, scale down to 2 mixologists (-$600), break-even at $75 ticket

2. **Sponsor acquisition failure:**
   - **Mitigation:** Start distillery outreach 8 weeks pre-event, multiple targets (209, St. George, local brands)
   - **Backup:** Increase ticket to $95 if no sponsor secured by 4 weeks out

3. **Weather (outdoor venue):**
   - **Mitigation:** Book for April-June (drier SF weather) vs. February
   - **Backup:** Flora Grubb has covered areas, can add tent rental ($800) if needed

### Operational Risks
1. **Station rotation chaos:**
   - **Mitigation:** Clear signage, volunteer station captains, 60-min timers
   - **Backup:** Reduce to 2 stations (herb garden + floral infusions) if 3 feels overwhelming

2. **Alcohol licensing:**
   - **Mitigation:** Secure permits 4 weeks in advance, caterer handles liquor license
   - **Backup:** Partner with licensed bar service vs. DIY alcohol setup

3. **Mixologist cancellations:**
   - **Mitigation:** Contract backup mixologists, confirm 1 week before event
   - **Backup:** Simplify format to demo + Q&A vs. hands-on if staff shortage
