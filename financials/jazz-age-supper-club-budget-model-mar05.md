## Why Premium Supper Club Model Doesn't Work

### 1. **Cost stack is too high**
- Venue: $3,000 (20% of ticket revenue)
- Food: $4,200 (52% of ticket revenue)
- Bar: $2,100 (26% of ticket revenue)
- **Food + Bar + Venue alone = 98% of ticket revenue**
- Jazz, host, entertainment, photo = no budget left

### 2. **60-guest capacity is too small**
- Fixed costs ($8,400) ÷ 60 guests = $140/person in fixed costs alone
- Need 100+ guests for economies of scale
- But intimate supper club vibe requires <80 guests
- **Catch-22: concept demands intimacy, but intimacy kills economics**

### 3. **Ticket price ceiling is real**
- SF supper club market: $100-150 for dinner + entertainment
- $145 is already at upper end
- Would need $225+ to break even (beyond market tolerance)
- Premium positioning alone doesn't justify 2x markup

### 4. **Competition from established venues**
- **Bix:** Already offers jazz + dinner nightly, $60-80 entrees
- **SFJAZZ:** Premier jazz venue, various price points
- **Mr. Tipple's / Local Edition:** Jazz clubs with food/cocktails
- Venn's value-add (storytelling, vintage vibe) isn't worth $100+ premium

### 5. **Sponsor acquisition is hard**
- Spirit brands typically sponsor $5-10K for big events (300+ guests)
- $2.5K for 60-person event is optimistic
- Would need 3 months lead time, multiple brand pitches
- Not reliable enough to build business model around

## Why Venue Partnership Model DOES Work

### 1. **Aligns incentives perfectly**
- **Bix gets:** New customers, filled seats, marketing exposure from Venn
- **Venn gets:** Zero venue/food/bar costs, focus on experience design
- **Guests get:** Premium supper club experience at accessible price

### 2. **Each party does what they're best at**
- Bix: Food, bar, jazz (their core competency)
- Venn: Marketing, experience curation, storytelling layer
- No overlap, no conflict

### 3. **Low capital risk for Venn**
- Total investment: $2,300 (host, props, marketing)
- vs. $15,900 for full production
- If event doesn't sell well → minimal loss

### 4. **Recurring series potential**
- "First Friday Jazz Supper at Bix" (monthly)
- Bix fills slow night (Fridays typically slower than Sat)
- Venn builds loyal audience for recurring revenue

### 5. **Proven model exists**
- Lazy Bear started as supper club, evolved to restaurant
- Many SF restaurants do "chef's table" partnerships
- Venue partnership = established revenue share model
