## Why Public Ticket Model Doesn't Work

### 1. **Yacht charter costs are prohibitive per guest**
- The Empress: $22,000 ÷ 150 = $147/person (yacht alone)
- Smaller yachts: $12,000 ÷ 60 = $200/person (WORSE economics)
- No economies of scale - charter cost is fixed regardless of guest count

### 2. **Catering + bar costs are high on yachts**
- Marine catering = premium pricing ($95-110/person for plated dinner)
- Open bar = $45-55/person (vs. $35-40 at land venues)
- Total F&B: $143-165/person (vs. $60-80 at typical Venn events)

### 3. **Market ticket ceiling is real**
- SF luxury event market: $150-200 max for social events
- Yacht gala at $225+ feels overpriced (not gala-level service, just location premium)
- Corporate galas charge $300-500 BUT include charity auction, keynote speakers, multi-course Michelin dining

### 4. **Sponsor acquisition is extremely difficult**
- Luxury brands want bigger audiences (300+ guests for activation ROI)
- Wine/champagne brands: $5-8K typical (not $15-25K needed)
- Maritime sponsors (boat companies): niche, small budgets
- Would need 3-4 sponsors at $8-10K each = 12-16 weeks of outreach, low success rate

### 5. **Ticket sales risk is massive**
- If only sell 100 tickets instead of 150 → loss increases to -$26K
- Yacht charter is committed upfront (deposit 30-60 days before)
- No refund if ticket sales fall short
- **Venn's capital at serious risk**

## Why Corporate Buyout Model DOES Work

### 1. **Single B2B sale vs. 150 individual ticket sales**
- One pitch meeting vs. months of marketing
- One contract vs. payment processing for 150 guests
- No ticket sales risk - guaranteed revenue

### 2. **Corporate budgets are higher**
- Tech companies: $400-600/person client entertainment budgets
- Finance firms: $500-800/person for HNW client events
- Venn's $433/person ($65K ÷ 150) = competitive pricing

### 3. **Turn-key value proposition**
- Corporation doesn't want to coordinate 7 vendors (yacht, catering, entertainment, etc.)
- Venn handles ALL logistics - client just shows up
- "White glove" production service commands 15-20% margin

### 4. **Recurring revenue potential**
- Once one company books, referrals to other firms
- Quarterly series: Q2 yacht gala, Q3 mansion party, Q4 holiday soirée
- Corporate clients want consistency - prefer working with same producer

### 5. **Lower marketing costs**
- B2B outreach via LinkedIn, direct sales, event planner networks
- No consumer advertising spend required
- Warm intro from one client leads to next booking

## Risk Assessment (Corporate Model)

### Business Risks
1. **No corporate buyer found:**
   - **Mitigation:** Start outreach 12+ weeks before desired event date, target 15+ companies
   - **Backup:** Defer concept indefinitely, no capital at risk if no buyer

2. **Corporate buyer cancels:**
   - **Mitigation:** 50% non-refundable deposit in contract, cancellation fees
   - **Backup:** Re-pitch to backup companies, or reschedule with original client

3. **Vendor costs exceed budget:**
   - **Mitigation:** Get fixed-price quotes in writing, 10% contingency buffer in pricing
   - **Backup:** Client contract specifies scope - out-of-scope requests are change orders

### Operational Risks
1. **Weather/sea conditions:**
   - **Mitigation:** Book May-September (calmest bay conditions), rain date policy
   - **Backup:** Yacht has indoor salons - event continues regardless of weather

2. **Yacht mechanical issues:**
   - **Mitigation:** Backup yacht on standby (contract with multiple charter companies)
   - **Backup:** Full refund + land venue pivot if yacht unable to sail

3. **Catering quality issues:**
   - **Mitigation:** Hire only yacht-experienced caterers, tastings before booking
   - **Backup:** Backup caterer on call, partial refund for quality issues
