# Urban Treasure Hunt - Event Research Brief
**Created:** February 25, 2026, 14:29 PST  
**Status:** Ideating  
**Researcher:** Vinny

## Event Concept Overview

**Theme:** City-wide scavenger hunt + puzzle-solving + exploration of hidden SF gems + social competition

**Format:** Teams race across San Francisco solving clues, completing challenges, discovering secret locations

**Target Audience:**
- 25-35 years old
- Adventure seekers, puzzle enthusiasts, competitive spirits
- Groups of friends, couples, coworkers (team activity)
- SF newcomers + longtime residents (different discovery angles)

**Capacity:** 100-150 guests (20-30 teams of 5)

**Duration:** 4 hours (2:00 PM - 6:00 PM, Saturday afternoon)

**Core Experience:**
1. **Team Check-In** (30 min, 2:00-2:30 PM): Registration, rules briefing, team photos
2. **The Hunt** (2.5 hours, 2:30-5:00 PM): Teams navigate SF solving clues, completing challenges
3. **Finish Line Celebration** (1 hour, 5:00-6:00 PM): Awards, stories, socializing, prizes

## Unique Differentiators

**Not a Typical Scavenger Hunt:**
- Clues lead to hidden SF locations (speakeasies, murals, historic sites tourists miss)
- Challenges combine: physical tasks + mental puzzles + photo submissions
- Local business partnerships (clues hidden at cafes, shops, landmarks)
- Real-time leaderboard (teams track progress via app/SMS)

**Discovery + Competition:**
- Residents learn new things about their city
- Newcomers explore beyond touristy areas
- Competition creates urgency and excitement
- Social element: Teams bond through shared adventure

## Route & Challenge Design

### Hunt Format

**10-12 Locations Across SF**
Teams visit locations in any order (flexible routing)

**Location Categories:**
1. **Hidden Gems** (4 stops): Speakeasies, secret gardens, murals, historic plaques
2. **Local Businesses** (3 stops): Independent bookstores, cafes, vintage shops (partnership opportunities)
3. **Landmarks** (3 stops): Public art, parks, viewpoints
4. **Final Challenge** (1 stop): All teams converge for final puzzle/task

**Challenge Types:**
- **Photo Challenges:** "Recreate this historic photo at the same location"
- **Riddle/Puzzle:** Solve clue to reveal next location
- **Local Interview:** "Ask a local to recommend their favorite hidden spot"
- **Creative Task:** "Create a team cheer at the top of this stairway"
- **Physical Challenge:** "Complete the Filbert Street steps in under 10 minutes"

**Point System:**
- Completing location challenges: 100 points each
- Bonus challenges: 50 points
- Speed bonuses: First 5 teams to finish get extra points (100/80/60/40/20)
- Photo creativity bonus: Judge awards 0-50 points per photo

### Sample Route (Example)

**Clue 1:** "Where three ladies once ruled the sky, find the art that reaches high" 
→ Answer: Coit Tower murals

**Clue 2:** "In the alley where cultures meet, jazz and dim sum can't be beat"
→ Answer: Ross Alley, Chinatown

**Clue 3:** "This bookstore cat knows every tale, find the orange one without fail"
→ Answer: The Booksmith (partnership opportunity)

[...continues with 7-9 more clues across SF neighborhoods]

**Final Challenge:** Teams reconvene at finishing location (Fort Mason, Golden Gate Park, Embarcadero Plaza) for final team puzzle

**Profit:** $9,000 - $7,150 = $1,850

- Revenue: $9,750
- Profit: $2,600

**At $55/ticket (120 guests - lower attendance):**

## Market Analysis

### Target Market Size (SF Bay Area)
- Escape room enthusiasts: Growing (50+ escape rooms in Bay Area)
- Amazing Race fans: Large (TV show model proven)
- Team activity seekers: Corporate + friend groups (huge market)
- SF explorers: Residents + newcomers always looking for new experiences

### Competition
- **City tours:** Educational but not competitive, not interactive ($40-80)
- **Escape rooms:** Puzzle-solving but indoor, no exploration ($30-50/person)
- **Bar crawls:** Social but not structured, no discovery element ($20-40)
- **Amazing Race-style events:** Exist but corporate-only, not public ($100-150/person corporate)

**Our Position:** Public version of corporate team-building events, discovery + competition hybrid

### Pricing Justification ($60/ticket)
- Includes: 4-hour adventure, team materials, challenges, prizes, guided discovery of SF
- Comparable: Escape rooms $40 + walking tour $40 = $80 value
- Corporate Amazing Race events: $100-150/person (ours is accessible, public)
- Team discount sweetens deal ($55/person for groups of 5)

## Risk Assessment

**HIGH RISKS:**
- **Weather dependency:** Outdoor event, rain/cold reduces enjoyment
- **Team separation/safety:** Teams navigating city independently
- **Technology failures:** App crashes, SMS delays, leaderboard errors

**MITIGATION:**
- Weather: Reschedule policy, rain-or-shine marketing (come prepared)
- Safety: Emergency contact cards, route monitors at key spots, team check-ins via app
- Tech: Backup manual system (Google Forms), test extensively pre-event

**MEDIUM RISKS:**
- Teams getting lost (provide clear clues, Google Maps links)
- Local business partner flakiness (have backup locations)
- Uneven team skill levels (balance clue difficulty)

**LOW RISKS:**
- Low attendance (unique concept, strong social media appeal)
- Permits (public parks generally allow gatherings under 150)
