# Urban Treasure Hunt - Event Research
**Generated:** February 25, 2026, 10:00 AM PST

**Concept:** City-wide adventure game with challenges, prizes, and social exploration

## Concept Overview
Transform San Francisco into a massive game board. Teams of 4-6 race across iconic neighborhoods solving puzzles, completing photo challenges, finding hidden checkpoints, and collecting clues. Mix of physical challenges (climb Filbert Steps), creative tasks (street performance), local discovery (find the best coffee in North Beach), and social media missions. Finish line celebration at a historic venue with prizes, photos, and community building.

**Target Audience:** 80-150 adventurous spirits, community builders, competitive teams (ages 22-40)  
**Season:** Year-round (weather-dependent challenges)  
**Duration:** 4 hours (2:30 PM kickoff, 6:30 PM finish line celebration)  
**Vibe:** Energetic, competitive, exploratory, Instagram-worthy adventures

## Event Format & Challenge Structure

### Hunt Zones (3-4 neighborhoods)
1. **North Beach** - Italian heritage, Coit Tower, cafes, landmarks
2. **Embarcadero/Ferry Building** - Waterfront, farmers market, piers
3. **Chinatown** - Culture, hidden alleys, fortune cookies, tea shops
4. **Mission** - Murals, taquerias, Dolores Park, street art

### Challenge Categories
1. **Photo Missions** - "Recreate a famous movie scene at Lombard Street"
2. **Local Discovery** - "Find the oldest cafe in North Beach, order an espresso, get receipt"
3. **Physical Challenges** - "Climb Filbert Steps, photo at top"
4. **Social Tasks** - "Convince a stranger to do the Carlton dance with you"
5. **Trivia/Puzzles** - "Decode this clue to find next checkpoint"
6. **Creative** - "Write a haiku about the Golden Gate Bridge, perform for a local"
7. **Food Missions** - "Try 3 dim sum items in Chinatown, rate them"

### Scoring System
- **Completed Challenges:** Points per challenge (easy=10, medium=25, hard=50)
- **Speed Bonus:** First teams to checkpoints get bonus points
- **Photo Quality:** Bonus points for creativity/humor
- **Social Sharing:** +10 points per Instagram story tag
- **Finish Time:** Arrival order at finish line (tiebreaker)

### Team Structure
- **Team Size:** 4-6 people
- **Total Teams:** 15-25 teams (75-150 total participants)
- **Registration:** Pre-event team formation OR day-of random assignment

## Key Insights

**DIY vs. Professional:** Professional scavenger hunt companies charge $2,000-3,500 but offer turnkey experience. DIY with GooseChase app costs $200-500 but requires significant design/testing time. **Recommendation:** Start with professional for V1, move to DIY for recurring events.

**Sponsor Goldmine:** Local businesses WANT foot traffic. Checkpoint sponsorships ($200-500 each) could generate $2,000-3,000. Outdoor brands (REI, Patagonia) natural fit for prizes.

**Finish Line Critical:** The post-hunt celebration is where community builds. Don't skimp on venue/catering - this is where photos, stories, and memories happen.

**Pricing Sweet Spot:** $50-60 is competitive for 4-hour adventure + celebration. Premium $75-80 requires elevated experience (better prizes, unique locations, celebrity host).

**Content Opportunity:** Teams will naturally create content during hunt. Instagram story challenges build social proof during event. Encourage hashtag use for organic reach.

**Scalability:** Unlike venue-constrained events, city-wide hunts can scale to 200+ participants easily. Margins improve significantly at 150+ tickets.
