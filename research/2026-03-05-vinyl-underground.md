# Vinyl Underground - Event Concept Research
**Date Generated:** March 5, 2026
**Concept:** Retro vinyl record listening party in speakeasy-style venue
**Target Audience:** Music lovers (28-45), vinyl collectors, audiophiles, retro enthusiasts
**Capacity Target:** 60-100 guests
**Budget Target:** $45-60/person

## Concept Overview
A throwback to the golden age of music discovery - guests gather in an intimate speakeasy setting to experience vinyl records on premium sound systems. Rotating DJ sets playing rare records, vinyl swap station, signature cocktails named after classic albums, and conversations about music history. Part listening party, part social mixer, all analog.

## Vendor Requirements

### DJ/Music Curator
- **Need:** Vinyl specialist DJs with rare record collections
- **Style:** Rotating sets - soul, funk, jazz, classic rock, hip-hop vinyl
- **Existing Contacts:**
  - Heart of Gold DJs (Info@heartofgolddjs.com) - 0.65 confidence (country focus, may not fit)
  - Bay Area Beats DJ (info@bayareabeats.com) - 0.65 confidence (need to verify vinyl capability)
- **New Contacts Needed:** SF vinyl DJs, record shop owners who DJ
- **Budget:** $800-1,500 for 4-hour event (2-3 DJs rotating)

### Sound System
- **Need:** High-quality turntables, vintage receivers, premium speakers
- **Options:**
  - Rent professional vinyl DJ setup
  - Partner with audiophile community (bring your own turntable showcase)
- **Budget:** $600-1,200 rental (if venue doesn't provide)

### Catering
- **Style:** Retro-inspired small bites, finger foods for mingling
- **Existing Contacts:**
  - Fraiche Catering (chi@fraichecater.com) - 0.6 confidence
  - 49 Square Catering (anthony@49square.com) - 0.7 confidence
- **Menu Ideas:** Classic cocktail party fare with vintage presentation
- **Budget:** $25-35/person (lighter food, more focus on drinks)

### Bar/Cocktails
- **Style:** Classic cocktails from different music eras (Old Fashioned, Martini, etc.)
- **Venue Consideration:** Many speakeasy venues include bar service
- **Signature Drinks:** Name after classic albums ("Dark Side of the Moon" cocktail, etc.)
- **Budget:** $20-30/person (2-3 drinks per guest)

### Photography
- **Style:** Moody, noir-inspired, vinyl close-ups, candid music moments
- **Existing Contacts:**
  - Scott Anderson - SF Event Photo (bookings@sfeventphoto.com) - 0.75 confidence

### Vinyl Swap Coordinator
- **Unique Element:** Guests bring records to trade
- **Need:** Someone to organize swap, verify condition, facilitate trades
- **Potential Partner:** Local record shop owner (Amoeba Music, Rasputin Records)
- **Budget:** $200-300 coordinator fee

## Key Insights
- **Niche Audience:** Vinyl culture is passionate but smaller than mainstream events
- **Venue Atmosphere Critical:** Speakeasy venues add story + authenticity
- **Monthly Series Potential:** Better as recurring event than one-off
- **Partner with Record Shops:** Built-in marketing + audience + credibility
- **Experiential Focus:** Less about big production, more about intimate music discovery
- **Alcohol-Friendly Crowd:** Bar revenue helps offset costs

## Unique Selling Points
1. **Analog Experience:** Screen-free social interaction around music
2. **Music Discovery:** Hear rare records you can't find on streaming
3. **Collector Community:** Meet other vinyl enthusiasts, trade records
4. **Storytelling:** Each DJ shares stories behind the records they play
5. **Intimate Scale:** Not a club night, a curated listening experience
