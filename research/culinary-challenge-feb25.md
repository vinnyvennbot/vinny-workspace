# Culinary Adventure Challenge - Event Research Brief
**Created:** February 25, 2026, 14:19 PST  
**Status:** Ideating  
**Researcher:** Vinny

## Event Concept Overview

**Theme:** Team-based culinary competition + mystery ingredient challenge + professional chef judging

**Format:** Part cooking competition, part social experience, part food festival

**Target Audience:**
- 25-40 years old
- Foodies, amateur home cooks, competitive spirits
- Groups of friends, couples, coworkers seeking unique team activity
- Fans of cooking shows (Top Chef, Chopped, MasterChef)

**Capacity:** 60-100 guests (12-20 teams of 5)

**Duration:** 3.5 hours (6:00 PM - 9:30 PM, Saturday)

**Core Experience:**
1. **Team Formation** (30 min): Guests form teams of 5, receive team name/color
2. **Mystery Box Challenge** (90 min): Teams receive mystery ingredients, create dish
3. **Judging Round** (30 min): Professional chef judges taste/score all dishes
4. **Social Hour** (60 min): Winners announced, appetizers/drinks, socializing
5. **Awards** (10 min): Winning team receives prizes

## Unique Differentiators

**Not a Cooking Class:**
- Competitive format (teams vs each other)
- Mystery ingredients create unpredictability
- Professional judging (real stakes)
- Social/spectator element (watching other teams)

**Social Competition Hybrid:**
- Cooking is core but not the only activity
- Socializing during prep, judging, awards
- Team bonding through collaboration
- Spectator-friendly (watching chaos unfold)

## Venue Research

**Ideal Venue Characteristics:**
- Commercial kitchen or multiple cooking stations (12-20 stations)
- Open layout (teams can see each other)
- Space for socializing separate from cooking area
- Indoor preferred (equipment needs)
- Parking/transit accessible

**Potential Venue Options:**

### Option 1: Commercial Kitchen Rental
**Examples:**
- The Civic Kitchen (SF commercial kitchen rental)
- Kitchen on Fire (cooking school with event space)
- Sur La Table (cooking class venues)

**Pros:** Professional equipment, built for cooking, insurance covered
**Cons:** Limited capacity, may not allow competition format
**Estimated Cost:** $1,500-3,000

### Option 2: Event Space + Portable Cooking Stations
**Examples:**
- Fort Mason Center (rent space + bring equipment)
- SoMa StrEat Food Park (outdoor with infrastructure)
- The Box SF (large event space)

**Pros:** Larger capacity, more flexible, can customize setup
**Cons:** Equipment rental required, more complex logistics
**Estimated Cost:** $2,000-4,000 venue + $1,500-2,500 equipment

### Option 3: Restaurant Partnership (Off-Hours)
**Examples:**
- Partner with restaurant closed Mondays/Tuesdays
- Use their kitchen + dining area
- They provide equipment/ingredients

**Pros:** Authentic kitchen, built-in equipment, possible food partnership
**Cons:** Limited availability, insurance concerns, smaller capacity
**Estimated Cost:** $1,000-2,500 + revenue share

## Vendor Requirements

### Professional Chef Judge (Priority: CRITICAL)
**What We Need:** 
- Local SF chef with credentials (restaurant, culinary school, TV experience)
- Engaging personality (can give feedback entertainingly)
- Available for 3.5 hours

**Judging Criteria:**
- Taste (40%)
- Presentation (30%)
- Creativity (20%)
- Use of mystery ingredients (10%)

**Potential Chefs to Contact:**
- Local restaurant chefs (Michelin-starred for credibility)
- Cooking school instructors
- Food media personalities in SF

**Estimated Cost:** $500-1,500 for 3.5 hours

### Equipment Rental (Priority: HIGH)
**If not using commercial kitchen:**
- 12-20 portable induction cooktops
- Cooking utensils (knives, cutting boards, pots, pans) × 20 teams
- Plates/bowls for dish presentation
- Refrigeration for ingredients
- Tables for cooking stations

**Estimated Cost:** $1,500-2,500 total

### Mystery Box Ingredients (Priority: CRITICAL)
**Format:** Each team gets identical mystery box

**Box Contents (Example):**
- 1 protein (chicken thighs, salmon, shrimp)
- 3 vegetables (seasonal - asparagus, mushrooms, bell peppers)
- 2 pantry staples (rice, pasta)
- 1 "challenge ingredient" (unique flavor - miso, tahini, harissa)
- Basics available to all: salt, pepper, oil, butter, stock

**Cost per Team:** $25-40 in ingredients × 20 teams = $500-800

### Beverages & Appetizers (Priority: MEDIUM)
**During Competition:** Water, soft drinks available

**Estimated Cost:** $20/person × 100 = $2,000

### Event Staff (Priority: HIGH)

- 2 Servers (social hour appetizers/drinks)
- 1 Photographer (capture competition moments)

**Estimated Cost:** $1,200-2,000 total

### Prizes (Priority: MEDIUM)

- $500 gift card to local restaurant OR

**Estimated Cost:** $500-700

## Financial Model (Preliminary)

### Estimated Costs (100 guests, 20 teams)

**Fixed Costs:**
- Venue: $2,500
- Equipment rental: $2,000
- Chef judge: $1,000
- Event MC/host: $500
- Staff (sous chefs, servers): $1,500
- Photographer: $400
- Prizes: $600
- Permits/insurance: $300
**Total Fixed:** $8,800

**Variable Costs per Person:**
- Mystery box ingredients: $8
- Social hour food/drink: $20
- Plates/utensils: $3
**Total Variable:** $31/person × 100 = $3,100

**Total Event Cost (100 guests):** $11,900

### Revenue Model

**Ticket Price:** $130-150 per person

**Revenue (100 guests @ $140):** $14,000

**Profit:** $14,000 - $11,900 = $2,100  
**Margin:** 15%

### Sensitivity Analysis

**At $150/ticket (100 guests):**
- Revenue: $15,000
- Profit: $3,100
- Margin: 21%

**At $130/ticket (80 guests - low attendance):**
- Revenue: $10,400
- Variable: $31 × 80 = $2,480
- Total cost: $11,280
- Loss: -$880

**Breakeven:** ~91 tickets at $130

**Optimal Pricing:** $140-150 range maximizes value perception while maintaining healthy margin

## Risk Assessment

**HIGH RISKS:**
- **Cooking skill variance:** Some teams may struggle, get frustrated
- **Safety concerns:** Multiple amateur cooks using knives/heat simultaneously
- **Logistics complexity:** Coordinating 20 cooking stations, timing, judging

**MITIGATION:**
- Pre-event: Clear safety briefing, sous chefs monitor stations
- Skill leveling: Mystery box designed for all skill levels (simple proteins, basic techniques)
- Rules: No dangerous techniques required, basics provided

**MEDIUM RISKS:**
- Weather (if outdoor venue)
- Equipment failure (induction cooktops, refrigeration)
- Judge no-show (have backup judge on standby)

**LOW RISKS:**
- Food allergies (teams choose how to prepare, can avoid allergens)
- Attendance (unique concept should sell well with proper marketing)
