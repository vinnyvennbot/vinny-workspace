## Concept Overview
Step back into 1920s San Francisco. Guests arrive in vintage attire (encouraged, not required) to an intimate speakeasy-style venue. Live jazz trio sets the mood as guests enjoy a multi-course prix fixe dinner with prohibition-era cocktails. Between courses: brief 1920s history storytelling, jazz education, vintage dance lesson. Ends with open dance floor and nightcap. Sophisticated, immersive, transportive experience for food lovers and history buffs alike.

**Target Audience:** 40-80 culture enthusiasts, jazz lovers, history buffs, romantics (ages 28-55)  
**Season:** Year-round (indoor venue, timeless appeal)  
**Duration:** 3.5 hours (6:30 PM arrival, 10:00 PM close)  
**Vibe:** Sophisticated, intimate, nostalgic, elegant, immersive

## Event Format & Experience Design

### Arrival (6:30-7:00 PM)
- **Welcome Cocktail:** Prohibition-era signature drink (Bee's Knees, Sidecar, French 75)
- **Jazz Trio:** Background music as guests arrive
- **Dress Code:** 1920s vintage encouraged, elegant cocktail attire minimum
- **Photo Op:** Vintage backdrop with 1920s props (feather boas, fedoras, pearls)

### Dinner Service (7:00-9:00 PM) - 3-Course Prix Fixe

**First Course** (7:00-7:30 PM)
- Classic 1920s appetizer (oysters Rockefeller, shrimp cocktail, or Caesar salad - invented 1924)
- **Interlude:** Brief welcome, 1920s SF history story (Prohibition, speakeasies, jazz age culture)

**Second Course** (7:30-8:15 PM)
- Entree options: Beef Wellington, roasted duck, lobster thermidor, or vegetarian option
- **Interlude:** Jazz education moment - musicians explain 1920s jazz style, demonstrate instrument

**Third Course** (8:15-9:00 PM)
- Classic dessert: Baked Alaska, crepes Suzette (tableside flambé drama!), or chocolate soufflé
- **Interlude:** Brief vintage dance lesson (Charleston basics, led by instructor)

### Dancing & Nightcaps (9:00-10:00 PM)
- **Open Dance Floor:** Jazz band uptempo set, guests dance
- **Final Cocktail Service:** Classic nightcaps available
- **Photo Time:** Professional photographer captures dance floor moments

### Entertainment Elements
1. **Live Jazz Trio/Quartet:** Period-appropriate 1920s jazz repertoire
2. **Storyteller/Host:** Brief historical vignettes between courses (5-7 min each)
3. **Dance Instructor:** 15-minute Charleston lesson
4. **Magician/Illusionist:** Optional roaming close-up magic (very 1920s)

## Key Insights

**High-Cost Experience:** 1920s immersive dinner requires significant investment (venue, food, cocktails, jazz, entertainment). This is a premium experience that's hard to price profitably at scale.

**Sponsor Critical:** Spirit brands (gin, champagne, whiskey) are natural fit. $3,000-5,000 bar sponsorship makes event viable. Vintage fashion partners add value through cross-promotion.

**Recurring Series Potential:** One-off supper club has high marketing costs. Monthly or quarterly series builds audience, amortizes fixed costs, creates anticipation. "First Friday Jazz Supper" brand.

**Venue Partnership Key:** Venues like Bix and Mr. Tipple's already have jazz + cocktails + ambiance. Partnership/revenue share model could reduce upfront venue costs.

**Premium Positioning Required:** This isn't a $60 ticket event. $125-190 range is appropriate for multi-course dinner + live jazz + entertainment + cocktails. Target affluent culture enthusiasts.

**Competition Analysis:** SF has jazz clubs (SFJAZZ, Bix) but few immersive 1920s dining experiences. Unique positioning as experiential history + culinary + music + social.

**Content Goldmine:** Vintage fashion, 1920s aesthetic, live jazz, cocktails = extremely Instagram-worthy. Partner with fashion creators for outfit inspiration content pre-event (drives ticket sales).
