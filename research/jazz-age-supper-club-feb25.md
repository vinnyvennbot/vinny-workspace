# Jazz Age Supper Club - Event Research Brief
**Created:** February 25, 2026, 14:14 PST  
**Status:** Ideating  
**Researcher:** Vinny

## Event Concept Overview

**Theme:** 1920s Prohibition-era supper club experience with live jazz, multi-course dinner, and vintage ambiance

**Target Audience:** 
- 30-50 years old
- Appreciates live music, fine dining, vintage aesthetics
- Couples, small groups seeking sophisticated night out
- Jazz enthusiasts, history lovers, experience collectors

**Capacity:** 80-120 guests (intimate supper club format)

**Duration:** 4 hours (7:00 PM - 11:00 PM, Friday or Saturday)

**Core Experience:**
- Live jazz trio/quartet performing throughout dinner
- Multi-course plated dinner (3-4 courses)
- Vintage 1920s cocktails and champagne service
- Period-appropriate dress encouraged (flapper dresses, suits, fedoras)
- Intimate candlelit atmosphere
- Optional: Brief dance instruction for swing/Charleston

**Style:** Traditional jazz, swing era, some modern jazz

## Financial Model (Preliminary)

### Estimated Costs (100 guests)
**Fixed Costs:**
- Venue: $2,000-4,000
- Live jazz band: $2,000-3,000
- Decor/rentals: $800-1,500
- Permits/insurance: $200-400
**Total Fixed:** $5,000-8,900

**Per-Person Variable Costs:**
- Catering (3 courses + service): $90
- Bar/cocktails (2-3 drinks): $40
- Linens/place settings: $8
**Total Variable:** $138/person × 100 = $13,800

**Total Event Cost (100 guests):** $18,800-22,700

### Revenue Model
**Ticket Price:** $200-250 per person

**Revenue (100 guests @ $225):** $22,500

**Profit Margin:**
- Low estimate: $22,500 - $22,700 = -$200 (break-even)
- High estimate: $22,500 - $18,800 = $3,700 profit

**Margin:** 0-16%

### Sensitivity Analysis
**At $250/ticket (100 guests):**
- Revenue: $25,000
- Profit: $2,300-6,200
- Margin: 9-25%

**At $225/ticket (80 guests - low attendance):**
- Revenue: $18,000
- Variable costs: $138 × 80 = $11,040
- Total: $16,040-19,940
- Loss: -$1,940 to +$1,960

**Breakeven:** ~85 tickets at $225

## Market Differentiation

**Unique Selling Points:**
1. Authentic multi-course dining (not just drinks/dancing)
2. Live jazz throughout (not DJ or background music)
3. Intimate capacity (exclusive feel)
4. Full immersive experience (dress, decor, music, food align)

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Most SF jazz venues: music-focused, minimal food
- Most upscale dinners: no live music or theme
- Speakeasy bars: drinks-focused, no formal dining
- **Our niche:** Full supper club experience rare in modern SF

- **Food quality critical:** Multi-course dinner must be exceptional at this price
- **Music sourcing:** Finding right jazz band crucial to ambiance

**MEDIUM RISKS:**

- Coordination complexity (band, caterer, service staff timing)

**MITIGATION STRATEGIES:**
- Secure venue with excellent reputation
- Hire experienced caterer with plated dinner expertise
