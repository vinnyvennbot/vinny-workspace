# Prohibition Speakeasy Night - Event Concept

**Generated:** Feb 23, 2026, 4:21 AM PST  
**Status:** Ideation - Awaiting Review

## Concept Overview
Transform a historic SF venue into a 1920s speakeasy with password entry, vintage cocktails, live jazz, and flapper fashion. Immersive experience with period-appropriate entertainment, secret doorways, and Prohibition-era storytelling.

## Target Audience
- Ages 25-40, professionals interested in SF history
- Vintage fashion enthusiasts, jazz lovers
- 150-200 guests (intimate but profitable scale)
- $70-90/person ticket (includes 2 cocktails + entertainment)

## Unique Selling Points
1. **Password Entry System** - Guests receive secret password via email, speakeasy-style entry
2. **Historic SF Venue** - Leverage actual Prohibition-era locations
3. **Vintage Cocktail Menu** - Period-accurate drinks (Bee's Knees, Southside, Mary Pickford)
4. **Live Jazz Band** - 4-piece ensemble, 1920s repertoire
5. **Costume Contest** - Best flapper/gangster outfit wins free ticket to next event
6. **SF History Element** - Tie to actual SF Prohibition stories (Barbary Coast, speakeasies)

## Venue Options (Research Needed)
1. **Bimbo's 365 Club** - Actually opened in 1931, authentic history
2. **The Speakeasy SF** - Already themed, but may lack authenticity 
3. **Foreign Cinema** - Hidden courtyard, vintage aesthetic
4. **Vesuvio Cafe** - Beat generation history, could work
5. **Palace Hotel** - Garden Court, historic ballroom

## Competitive Analysis
- **Prohibition-themed bars** exist but are permanent installations
- **One-off speakeasy events** rare in SF currently
- **Gatsby parties** common, but speakeasy angle different (more intimate, mysterious)
- **Differentiator:** Password entry + SF history storytelling + authentic venue
