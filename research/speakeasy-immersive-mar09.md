# Event Concept Research: The Blind Tiger Speakeasy
**Date Created:** March 9, 2026  
**Researcher:** Vinny  
**Status:** Ideating

## Concept Overview
**Theme:** Prohibition-era speakeasy with immersive theater elements  
**Format:** Secret entrance, password entry, period costumes encouraged, live jazz, vintage cocktails, actor-led storylines  
**Experience:** Transport guests to 1920s SF underground bar with characters, plot twists, authentic prohibition drinks  
**Target Audience:** Theater enthusiasts, history buffs, costume lovers (25-45)  
**Capacity:** 80-120 guests  
**Price Point:** $75-95/person  
**Revenue Model:** Ticket sales + vintage cocktail bar, period-appropriate brand sponsors

## Venue Options (3+ researched)

### 1. The Mint (Basement Vaults) - PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION
- **Location:** Downtown SF
- **Type:** Historic US Mint building with underground gold vaults
- **Capacity:** Vaults can accommodate 80-120 standing/seated mix
- **Pros:** PERFECT speakeasy vibe (actual underground vaults), historic landmark, built-in mystique
- **Estimated Cost:** $4,000-6,000 venue rental
- **Contact:** events@themint.org
- **Status:** Researching
- **Why Perfect:** Literal underground setting, gold vault history = prohibition-era bank heist vibes

### 2. Flood Mansion - Basement/Lower Level
- **Location:** Pacific Heights
- **Type:** 1906 mansion with period-appropriate architecture
- **Capacity:** 80-100 guests in lower level spaces
- **Pros:** Historic mansion, multiple rooms for "secret" areas, upscale setting
- **Estimated Cost:** $3,500-5,000 venue rental
- **Contact:** info@floodmansion.org
- **Status:** Researching

### 3. Bimbo's 365 Club
- **Location:** North Beach
- **Type:** Historic nightclub (opened 1931, prohibition-adjacent era)
- **Capacity:** Main room 650+, smaller rooms available
- **Pros:** AUTHENTIC 1930s venue, built-in stage/sound, real speakeasy history
- **Cons:** May be too large unless we use smaller room
- **Estimated Cost:** $3,000-5,000 for private room rental
- **Contact:** Already have Bimbo's in database
- **Status:** Researching (existing relationship)

### 4. Undisclosed Private Location Strategy
- **Concept:** Don't reveal venue until 48h before event
- **Marketing:** "Secret SF location revealed to ticket holders only"
- **Pros:** Builds mystique, flexibility to use various basement/hidden venues
- **Implementation:** Use warehouse space, private club, or undisclosed restaurant basement

## Vendor Requirements

### Immersive Theater Company/Actors
**Need:** 5-8 actors playing prohibition-era characters (speakeasy owner, flapper, bootlegger, detective, etc.)
- **Option 1:** SF immersive theater companies (research: Boxcar Theatre style)

- **Estimated Cost:** $2,000-3,500 (actors, costumes, script development)

**Need:** 4-5 piece jazz band playing 1920s standards (swing, ragtime, hot jazz)
- **Option 1:** SF jazz musicians specializing in vintage

## Experience Design Details

### Pre-Event
- Guests receive "secret password" via email 48h before
- Encouraged (not required) to wear period costume
- Mystery/intrigue marketing: "Find the hidden entrance..."

### Arrival
- Unmarked entrance with doorman checking passwords
- Actor greeting: "You here for the... you know?"
- Immediate immersion starts at door

### During Event
- 3-4 storyline moments throughout night (actor scenes)
- Guests can interact with characters
- Live jazz provides authentic soundtrack
- Vintage cocktails served prohibition-style
- Optional: Surprise "raid" by actors playing prohibition agents (comedic)

### Departure
- "Back door exit" for speakeasy authenticity
- Period photo booth as souvenir
