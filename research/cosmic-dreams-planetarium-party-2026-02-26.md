# Cosmic Dreams: Planetarium Party - Event Concept Research
**Generated:** February 26, 2026, 10:00 AM PST  
**Status:** Ideating (Awaiting Approval)  
**Theme:** Science meets nightlife under the stars

## Concept Overview
An immersive party experience inside a planetarium, combining astronomy shows with DJ sets, cosmic cocktails, and interactive science installations. Guests dance under projected galaxies, learn about constellations between DJ sets, and experience the universe from the ultimate venue. Think: Cosmos meets club culture.

**Target Audience:** 200-400 curious professionals (25-40) who love science, unique experiences, and intellectual socializing  
**Ideal Season:** Year-round (indoor venue)  
**Experience Goals:** Wonder + social connection, intellectual stimulation meets party energy, Instagram-worthy cosmic content

---

## Venue Options (Limited but Spectacular)

### Option 1: California Academy of Sciences - Morrison Planetarium ⭐️
- **Location:** 55 Music Concourse Dr, Golden Gate Park, San Francisco, CA 94118
- **Capacity:** 200-300 guests (planetarium dome seats ~300)
- **Vibe:** World's largest all-digital dome, aquarium + rainforest venue access, iconic SF institution
- **Estimated Cost:** $15,000-$30,000 venue rental (includes access to aquarium, rainforest during event)
- **Pros:** DREAM VENUE, built-in planetarium shows, multiple spaces, professional event staff
- **Cons:** High cost, limited availability (popular venue), strict rules on alcohol/food
- **Contact:** calacademy.org/plan-an-event
- **Notes:** Planetarium can be booked as standalone or as part of larger Academy buyout
- **Ideal Format:** Planetarium show → party in aquarium/terraces → return to planetarium for finale

### Option 2: Lawrence Hall of Science (UC Berkeley)
- **Location:** 1 Centennial Dr, Berkeley, CA 94720
- **Capacity:** 150-250 guests (planetarium + surrounding spaces)
- **Vibe:** East Bay hilltop location, bay views, academic setting, smaller/more intimate
- **Estimated Cost:** $8,000-$15,000 venue rental
- **Pros:** Lower cost than CAS, flexible spaces, stunning views, science installations
- **Cons:** Outside SF proper (transportation), smaller planetarium, less iconic
- **Contact:** lawrencehallofscience.org private events
- **Notes:** Strong backup if CAS unavailable

### Option 3: Chabot Space & Science Center (Oakland)
- **Location:** 10000 Skyline Blvd, Oakland, CA 94619
- **Capacity:** 200-350 guests (multiple theaters + outdoor spaces)
- **Vibe:** Hilltop location, telescopes, multiple planetarium theaters, observatory vibes
- **Estimated Cost:** $10,000-$20,000 venue rental
- **Pros:** Multiple planetarium options, telescope viewing, lower cost than CAS
- **Cons:** Oakland location (30 min from SF), less central for SF crowd
- **Contact:** chabotspace.org venue rentals
- **Notes:** Outdoor terrace with telescope viewing = unique add-on

### Option 4: Portable Planetarium + Industrial Warehouse (DIY Model)
- **Location:** SoMa/Mission warehouse (TBD)
- **Capacity:** 150-300 guests
- **Vibe:** Custom-built experience, full creative control, underground event aesthetic
- **Estimated Cost:** $3,000-$6,000 venue + $8,000-$15,000 portable planetarium rental + setup
- **Pros:** Lower total cost, creative freedom, can do multiple events with rented equipment
- **Cons:** Complex logistics, less "real" planetarium magic, requires extensive setup
- **Contact:** Research portable planetarium rental companies (they exist for schools/events)
- **Notes:** Backup option if budget-constrained or major venues unavailable

## Vendor Categories Needed

### Entertainment
- **DJ:** Specializing in ambient/electronic/cosmic vibes (not aggressive EDM - think Bonobo, ODESZA energy)
- **Planetarium Operator:** If CAS/Chabot, their staff runs shows; if DIY, need operator
- **VJ/Visual Artist:** Custom cosmic visuals synced to music (if not using planetarium projection)
- **Science Communicator:** Brief astronomy talks between DJ sets (optional - adds educational layer)

### Production
- **Lighting:** Minimal (planetarium = dark) - LED wristbands, glow-in-dark elements, blacklight accents
- **Sound System:** Professional setup (planetariums have acoustics - need expertise)
- **Interactive Installations:** VR space stations, telescope viewing areas, constellation mapping stations
- **Projection Mapping:** If using non-planetarium spaces for additional rooms

### Catering
- **Theme:** "Cosmic Cocktails" - Galaxy martinis, Nebula cocktails, Stardust shots
- **Food:** Passed hors d'oeuvres (finger foods - dark venue = easy-to-eat important)
- **Bar:** Creative cosmic-themed drink menu with dry ice effects
- **Dietary:** Space-themed vegan options ("Plant-Based Planets")

### Photography
- **360° Camera:** Capture dome projections + crowd reactions
- **Roaming Photographer:** Guests under galaxy projections = incredible shots
- **Slow-Mo Booth:** Guests "floating" in zero gravity (green screen effects)

### Logistics
- **Transportation:** Shuttle from downtown SF to venue (if CAS/Chabot)
- **Coat Check:** If nighttime event in winter
- **Accessibility:** Ensure planetarium seating accommodates all guests

## Experience Flow (Sample Event)

**7:00 PM - Arrival & Cosmic Cocktails**  
Welcome drinks, interactive science installations, telescope viewing (if outdoor space)

**7:30 PM - Opening Planetarium Show**  
20-minute guided journey through the cosmos (professional narrator + visuals)

**8:00 PM - DJ Set Begins Under the Stars**  
Dance floor under projected galaxies, ambient/electronic music

**9:00 PM - Intermission: Science Talk**  
5-minute fascinating astronomy fact (black holes, exoplanets, etc.) - keeps it intellectual

**9:15 PM - DJ Set Continues**  
Energy builds, full dance party under the cosmos

**10:00 PM - Finale Planetarium Show**  
10-minute synchronized visual finale as party peaks

**10:15 PM - After-Party (Aquarium/Terraces if CAS)**  
Wind down with drinks, mingling, photo ops
