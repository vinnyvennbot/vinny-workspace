# Underground Supper Club: Chef's Table Series - Event Concept Research
**Generated:** February 26, 2026, 10:00 AM PST  
**Status:** Ideating (Awaiting Approval)  
**Theme:** Intimate multi-course culinary adventure in secret locations

## Concept Overview
A recurring series of intimate chef's table dinners in unexpected SF locations. Each event features a different rising chef, secret location revealed 24 hours before, and a themed multi-course menu. Think: exclusive culinary experience meets social adventure meets SF's underground dining culture.

**Target Audience:** 30-60 food-obsessed professionals (28-45) willing to pay premium for unique culinary experiences  
**Ideal Season:** Year-round (indoor focus)  
**Experience Goals:** Culinary discovery, intimate social connection, sense of exclusivity, chef interaction

---

## Venue Options (Rotating Concept)

### Option 1: Private Lofts/Apartments (Residential Supper Club Model)
- **Location:** Mission, SoMa, Nob Hill (varies per event)
- **Capacity:** 20-40 guests (intimate dining)
- **Vibe:** Secret address, residential intimacy, dinner party atmosphere
- **Estimated Cost:** $500-$2,000 per event (host compensation/rental)
- **Pros:** Authentic underground vibe, low venue cost, flexible locations
- **Cons:** Permitting gray area, neighbor noise concerns, limited kitchen facilities
- **Contact:** Partner with homeowners/loft owners willing to host
- **Notes:** This is how SF supper clubs historically operated (Hi Felicia, Canvas Underground models)

### Option 2: Lazy Bear (Established Supper Club Model)
- **Location:** Mission District
- **Capacity:** 40-60 guests
- **Vibe:** Professional supper club, communal seating, ticketed reservations
- **Estimated Cost:** $3,000-$6,000 venue rental (or revenue share model)
- **Pros:** Proven model, licensed kitchen, established reputation
- **Cons:** Less "underground" feel, higher cost, less exclusivity
- **Contact:** Lazy Bear events team
- **Notes:** Study their model - they transitioned from underground to licensed

### Option 3: 25 Lusk Private Events Space
- **Location:** SoMa
- **Capacity:** 30-80 guests (flexible spaces)
- **Vibe:** Industrial-chic, high ceilings, open kitchen visibility
- **Estimated Cost:** $4,000-$8,000 venue rental
- **Pros:** Professional venue, licensed, chef-friendly kitchen, private spaces
- **Cons:** More corporate feeling, higher cost reduces margins
- **Contact:** 25lusk.com private events

### Option 4: Warehouse/Unusual Spaces (Pier, Gallery, etc.)
- **Location:** Varies (Embarcadero piers, SoMa galleries, rooftops)
- **Capacity:** 40-100 guests
- **Vibe:** Unexpected locations, site-specific experiences
- **Estimated Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 venue + $1,000-$3,000 temporary kitchen setup
- **Pros:** True "secret location" magic, Instagram-worthy, flexible
- **Cons:** Kitchen logistics challenging, permits required, higher setup cost
- **Contact:** Research via Canvas Underground model (they did pier events)

## Chef Partnership Model

### Chef Selection Criteria
- **Rising talent:** Not yet Michelin-starred but on the way (hungry, creative, affordable)
- **Unique perspective:** Immigrant stories, fusion concepts, experimental techniques
- **Personality:** Charismatic, willing to interact with guests between courses
- **Availability:** Willing to do one-off events (side hustle for restaurant chefs)

### Compensation Models
**Option A - Flat Fee:** $1,500-$3,000 per event (guaranteed payment)  
**Option B - Revenue Share:** 30-40% of ticket sales (chef takes upside risk)  
**Option C - Hybrid:** $1,000 base + 25% of sales above break-even

### Potential Chef Partners (Research Needed)
- Reach out to sous chefs at top SF restaurants (Lazy Bear, State Bird, Benu)
- Contact SF Cooking School instructors
- Search Instagram/TikTok for emerging SF chef content creators
- Partner with immigrant chef collectives (Filipino, Indian, Mexican cuisines)

## Vendor Categories Needed

### Culinary
- **Chef:** Rotating guest chef per event (core partnership)
- **Kitchen Staff:** 1-2 assistants for chef (prep, plating, service)
- **Sommelier/Beverage Director:** Wine pairings for each course (optional premium add-on)

### Service
- **Servers:** 2-4 professional servers (1:15 guest ratio)
- **Host:** Front-of-house coordinator (greetings, flow, storytelling)

### Production (Minimal - Intimate Setting)
- **Lighting:** Warm Edison bulbs, candles (romantic ambiance)
- **Table Settings:** Quality linens, glassware, plates (if venue lacks)
- **Music:** Curated playlist (subtle background jazz/acoustic)

### Logistics
- **Portable Kitchen:** If non-traditional venue (hot plates, prep tables, refrigeration)
- **Permits:** Health department permits for one-off events (research requirements)
- **Insurance:** Event liability + food service coverage

## Experience Flow (Sample Event)

**6:00 PM - Secret Location Reveal**  
Guests receive address via email/text 24 hours prior

**6:30 PM - Arrival & Welcome Cocktail**  
Champagne or signature cocktail, introductions

**7:00 PM - Chef Introduction**  
Chef shares their story, menu inspiration, ingredient sourcing

**7:15 PM - Course 1**  
Amuse-bouche + wine pairing introduction

**7:30-9:30 PM - Courses 2-6**  
Multi-course progression, chef table visits between courses

**9:30 PM - Dessert & Coffee**  
Sweet finale, digestifs

**10:00 PM - Lingering**  
Guests socialize, exchange contacts, chef mingles

## Recurring Series Strategy

### Event Frequency
- **Phase 1 (Test):** 1 event/month for 3 months (validate demand)
- **Phase 2 (Growth):** 2 events/month with rotating chefs/themes
- **Phase 3 (Scale):** Weekly events across multiple venues

### Membership Model (Future)
- **Supper Club Membership:** $500/year for priority access + discounts
- **Chef's Circle:** $1,200/year for 6 guaranteed seats + meet-the-chef events
- **Founder's Table:** $3,000/year for 12 events + private chef consultations

### Theme Rotation Ideas
- **Regional Cuisines:** Filipino, Ethiopian, Peruvian, Georgian
- **Technique-Focused:** Fermentation, Molecular, Live-Fire
- **Story-Driven:** Immigrant Chef Series, Women in Culinary, Farm-to-Table Journeys

## Unique Selling Points
- **Exclusivity:** Limited seats, secret locations, invitation-only feel
- **Chef Access:** Direct interaction with rising culinary talent
- **Discovery:** Experience cuisines/techniques not available in restaurants
- **Community:** Small groups foster real connections over shared meals
- **Storytelling:** Every event has narrative arc (chef's journey, ingredients, techniques)

## Legal/Risk Considerations

- **Insurance:** Event liability + food service coverage mandatory

- **Food safety:** Certified kitchen or temporary food facility permit required
