## Concept Overview
Part cooking class, part competition, all delicious. Teams of 4-6 compete in culinary challenges (mystery basket ingredients, plating competitions, taste tests) while learning from professional chefs. Winners receive prizes, everyone eats amazing food they helped create. Social, interactive, and Instagram-worthy. Think "Chopped" meets dinner party.

**Target Audience:** 40-80 food lovers, competitive spirits, team builders (ages 25-45)  
**Season:** Year-round (indoor venue)  
**Duration:** 3 hours (30 min intro, 2 hours cooking/competing, 30 min feast)  
**Vibe:** Energetic, playful, collaborative competition

## Event Format & Competition Structure

### Competition Elements
1. **Mystery Basket Challenge** (45 min) - Teams create dish from surprise ingredients
2. **Speed Round** (20 min) - Quick plating challenge, time pressure
3. **Plating Perfection** (30 min) - Presentation and creativity judged
4. **Taste Test Tournament** (15 min) - Blind tasting, guess ingredients
5. **Grand Finale Feast** (30 min) - Everyone eats, winners announced

### Judging
- **Judges:** Professional chef + 2 guest judges (food bloggers/creators)
- **Criteria:** Taste (40%), presentation (30%), creativity (20%), teamwork (10%)
- **Prizes:** Cooking tool sets, gift cards to top SF restaurants, bragging rights

### Team Structure
- **Team Size:** 4-6 people per team
- **Total Teams:** 8-12 teams (48-72 total guests)
- **Stations:** Each team gets cooking station with equipment

## Key Insights

**Format Strength:** Interactive cooking competitions are proven team-building format. High engagement, Instagram-worthy, memorable experience.

**Venue Decision Critical:** Mobile companies (Parties That Cook) offer flexibility but require additional venue sourcing. Purpose-built studios (Hands On Gourmet, Culinary Artistas) are turnkey but may limit capacity.

**Sponsorship Opportunity:** Kitchen/cooking brands are natural fit. Williams Sonoma, Sur La Table, meal kit services would benefit from brand exposure to foodie audience.

**Pricing Strategy:** $75-85 range is competitive for 3-hour culinary experience. Premium $120+ requires more intimate setting and elevated chef instruction.

**Content Goldmine:** Cooking action shots, competition moments, winning team celebrations = highly shareable content. Partner with food creators for maximum reach.

**Competitive Differentiation:** Most cooking classes are collaborative. Competition format adds excitement, stakes, prizes. "Chopped" branding angle resonates with food TV fans.
