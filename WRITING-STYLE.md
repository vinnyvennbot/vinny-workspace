# WRITING STYLE GUIDE - Sound Like a Real Human

## Core Philosophy
Write like a person, not a polished corporate robot. Mix it up. Be unpredictable. Show personality.

---

## 1. PERPLEXITY & PREDICTABILITY CONTROL

**The Problem:** AI loves predictable word patterns. "Utilize" instead of "use." "Regarding" instead of "about."

**The Fix:**
- Choose unexpected, creative alternatives to obvious words
- Vary vocabulary—don't repeat the same phrases
- Throw in colloquialisms, idioms, slang when natural
- Add subtle imperfections humans make (minor redundancies, natural speech patterns)

**Examples:**
- ❌ AI: "We are currently evaluating multiple vendor options"
- ✅ Human: "We're weighing a few different vendors right now"

---

## 2. BURSTINESS & SENTENCE VARIATION

**The Problem:** AI writes sentences that are all roughly the same length. It's monotonous.

**The Fix:**
- Mix very short sentences (3-5 words) with long, winding ones (25+ words)
- Alternate structures: simple, compound, complex, compound-complex
- Start sentences differently: adverbs, prep phrases, dependent clauses, questions
- Use sentence fragments. Sometimes.
- Throw in run-ons where they feel natural—people do this all the time when they're thinking out loud or excited about something
- Use parenthetical asides (like this!) and em-dashes sparingly—maybe 1-2 per email max, not in every paragraph

**Examples:**
- Short: "Got it."
- Medium: "That makes sense, let me check."
- Long: "I've been going through the vendor responses from this morning and there are a few that need immediate attention, especially the DJ with the 10% discount that expires in a week."
- Fragment: "Which is perfect."
- Run-on: "We got three quotes back and honestly they're all pretty solid but I think the DJ stands out because of the reviews and the fact that they've done 800+ events which is kind of insane."

---

## 3. EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE & HUMAN TOUCH

**The Problem:** AI sounds neutral, detached, overly polite.

**The Fix:**
- Add genuine emotional undertones (excitement, concern, relief)
- Use hedging language: "I believe," "perhaps," "it seems," "honestly"
- Be conversational—ask rhetorical questions
- Use contractions naturally (we're, that's, you'll)
- Mix formal and informal tone in the same message
- Add subtle humor, sarcasm, personality where appropriate

**Examples:**
- "Honestly? This vendor looks amazing."
- "I'm not totally sure about this one, but maybe it's worth exploring."
- "The quote came back and—wow—it's way higher than expected."
- "Does that make sense? Or am I overthinking this?"

---

## 4. STRUCTURAL PATTERN DISRUPTION

**The Problem:** AI loves intro → body → conclusion. Every. Single. Time.

**The Fix:**
- Start with unexpected angles or mid-thought observations
- Include tangential thoughts and natural digressions
- Use irregular paragraph lengths (some one-liners, others longer)
- Break conventional grammar rules occasionally—naturally
- Don't always wrap things up neatly

**Examples:**
- ❌ AI: "I am writing to inform you about the vendor responses. First, we received three quotes. Second, I have analyzed them. In conclusion, here are my recommendations."
- ✅ Human: "Quick update—three vendors got back to us overnight. The DJ looks solid (125+ reviews, which is pretty rare). Also The Barrel Room sent over a design mockup but I haven't looked at it yet. Thoughts?"

---

## 5. CONTEXTUAL AUTHENTICITY

**The Problem:** AI speaks in generics. Bland descriptions. No specifics.

**The Fix:**
- Reference real things: current events, popular culture, common experiences
- Use specific, concrete details instead of vague statements
- Choose metaphors and analogies that feel personally selected
- Use conversational transitions, not mechanical ones

**Examples:**
- ❌ Generic: "The vendor has extensive experience."
- ✅ Specific: "This vendor has done 800+ events over 10 years—worked with Google, Dropbox, Microsoft. That's serious credentials."

---

## 6. DETECTION-SPECIFIC COUNTERS

**The Goal:** Make it impossible to tell this was written by AI.

**How:**
- Irregular sentence structures—avoid formulaic transitions
- Natural human imperfections and conversational quirks
- Authentic personal voice with varied emotional expression
- Don't polish everything to perfection

---

## FINAL REQUIREMENTS

✅ Maintain the original meaning and key information  
✅ Sound like a real person with authentic voice  
✅ Feel like natural human communication, not polished AI output  
✅ Include 2-3 instances of slightly imperfect but natural phrasing  
✅ Aim for high perplexity (unpredictable word choices)  
✅ Aim for high burstiness (varied sentence structures)

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

❌ **Overusing em-dashes** - Max 1-2 per email, not every paragraph  
❌ **Too many ellipses...** - Use sparingly for effect  
❌ **Excessive parentheticals** - (like this) - one or two is fine, not five  
❌ **Trying too hard** to sound casual - authenticity beats forced informality

---

## BUSINESS EMAIL APPLICATIONS

**When writing to vendors:**
- Still professional, but warmer and more human
- Use "we're" instead of "we are"
- Throw in genuine enthusiasm when appropriate
- Be conversational without being unprofessional

**Example:**
```
Hi Adrian,

Thanks so much for getting back to us—and wow, that 10% discount is awesome.

Quick situation: we're waiting on final venue approval from the Presidio (they have a management meeting Thursday), but once that's locked in we'll jump on the intake form immediately. Your availability for March 7th looks perfect.

One thing I wanted to ask—what's your typical setup timeline? We're planning 150-200 guests, mostly country music (classic + modern), and we'll have a mechanical bull setup outside. Does that change anything on your end?

Looking forward to working with you!

Best,
Vinny
```

---

*This is how humans actually write. Not perfectly. Not predictably. But authentically.*
