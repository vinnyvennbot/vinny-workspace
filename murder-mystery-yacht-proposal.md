## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Key Findings

**FEASIBILITY: HIGH** - A 400-person murder mystery yacht party in San Francisco Bay is highly feasible with established vendors and proven track record.

**Critical Success Factor:** Venn has already successfully executed a 400-person yacht party ("Boots on Deck" - Summer 2025), demonstrating operational capability at this scale.

**Top Recommendations:**
1. **Venue:** City Experiences (formerly Hornblower) - San Francisco Spirit yacht (400 capacity)
2. **Production:** The Murder Mystery Company or Epic Immersive for theatrical elements
3. **Lead Time Required:** 4-6 months minimum for coordination
4. **Estimated Budget Range:** $85,000 - $140,000 ($210-$350 per person)

### Risk Assessment

**LOW RISK:**
- Proven venue capacity (400+ guests)
- Multiple vendor options in all categories
- SF Bay weather generally favorable
- Venn's prior experience with large yacht events

**MEDIUM RISK:**
- Theatrical production coordination with marine environment
- Sound/lighting integration on water
- Boarding logistics for 400 people (requires 45-60 minutes)

**HIGH ATTENTION NEEDED:**
- Coast Guard safety compliance (life jackets for 400+ people)
- Emergency evacuation protocols
- Weather contingency planning
- Vendor coordination timeline

## 5. LOGISTICS ANALYSIS

### BOARDING PROTOCOLS

**Timeline for 400 Guests:**
- **Check-in Setup:** 30 minutes before boarding
- **Boarding Duration:** 45-60 minutes (recommended)
- **Staggered Entry:** Multiple gangways if available
- **Staff Required:** 6-8 check-in staff, 4 security personnel

**Boarding Process:**
1. Ticket/RSVP verification
2. ID check (21+/alcohol service)
3. Safety briefing handout
4. Wristband/identification for "suspect" roles
5. Directed to assigned deck/area

**Optimizations:**
- Pre-registration with QR codes
- Multiple entry points
- Designated "suspect" pre-boarding for actor coordination
- Welcome drink station to encourage flow

---

### SAFETY PROTOCOLS

**US Coast Guard Requirements (400+ passengers):**

**Mandatory Equipment:**
- Coast Guard-approved life jackets for every person (400+)
- Life jackets must be readily accessible and properly stored
- Visual demonstration of life jacket location required
- Emergency lighting and exit signs
- Fire extinguishers and suppression systems
- First aid stations

**Vessel Certification:**
- Must be Coast Guard certified for passenger capacity
- Commercial vessel insurance
- Licensed captain and crew
- Regular safety inspections

**Staff Requirements:**
- Trained safety personnel
- Crew briefed on emergency procedures
- Medical personnel on standby (recommended for 400+)
- Coast Guard compliance officer review

**Weather Monitoring:**
- Real-time weather tracking
- Communication with Coast Guard Station San Francisco
- Designated weather decision maker
- Return-to-dock protocols

---

### EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

**Medical Emergencies:**
- First aid station on board (required)
- Communication with Coast Guard for medevac if needed
- Paramedic on-site recommended for 400 guests
- Nearest return dock identified (Pier 3, Berkeley Marina)

**Vessel Emergency:**
- Coast Guard immediate contact protocols
- Life raft deployment procedures (if equipped)
- Evacuation drills for crew
- Guest muster stations clearly marked

**Event-Specific Risks:**
- Intoxication management (security staff)
- Crowd control for theatrical performances
- Slip/fall prevention (wet decks)
- Missing guest protocols

---

### SF BAY-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

**Weather Factors:**
- **Best Months:** May-October (warmer, less wind)
- **Challenging Months:** November-April (rain, cold, wind)
- **Fog:** Common summer evenings (plan indoor backup)
- **Wind:** Can be strong near Golden Gate Bridge

**Route Planning:**
- **Scenic Route:** Under Golden Gate, around Alcatraz, past Bay Bridge
- **Calm Waters:** Stay in central bay, avoid straits
- **Duration:** 3-hour cruise typical (6:00 PM - 9:00 PM)
- **Sunset Timing:** Plan murder mystery reveal for golden hour

**Docking Logistics:**
- **Primary:** Pier 3, San Francisco (City Experiences home base)
- **Alternatives:** Berkeley Marina, Jack London Square (Oakland)
- **Parking:** Coordinate shuttle or ride-share pickup zones
- **Accessibility:** ADA-compliant boarding ramps required

## 10. RISK ASSESSMENT

### OPERATIONAL RISKS

#### **Weather-Related Risks** - MEDIUM
**Risk:** Fog, wind, rain impacts outdoor experience and guest comfort

**Mitigation:**
- Book summer months (May-October) for best weather
- City Experiences yachts have climate-controlled interiors (400 capacity indoors)
- Have indoor-only contingency for murder mystery if needed
- Weather monitoring 7 days before with decision point 48 hours out
- Insurance for cancellation due to unsafe conditions

**Likelihood:** Low (SF Bay summers are generally favorable)  
**Impact if occurs:** Medium (event moves indoors, still happens)

---

#### **Boarding Delays** - MEDIUM
**Risk:** 400 people take longer than expected to board, delaying departure

**Mitigation:**
- **Staggered entry times** (VIP/suspects early, general 30 min later)
- **Multiple check-in stations** (6-8 staff)
- **QR code digital tickets** for fast scanning
- **Buffer time:** Start boarding 60 minutes before departure
- **Communication:** Clear instructions to guests beforehand

**Likelihood:** Medium  
**Impact if occurs:** Low-Medium (delayed start, compressed schedule)

---

#### **Technical Failures** - LOW-MEDIUM
**Risk:** Sound system, lighting, or AV equipment fails during performance

**Mitigation:**
- **Backup equipment:** Redundant mics and speakers
- **Technical operator on-site** throughout event
- **Pre-event testing:** Full tech rehearsal on yacht
- **Simple backup plan:** Actors can perform without mics if needed (project voices)

**Likelihood:** Low (professional equipment and operators)  
**Impact if occurs:** Medium (audience experience degraded but event continues)

---

#### **Medical Emergency** - LOW
**Risk:** Guest injury, illness, or intoxication requiring medical attention

**Mitigation:**
- **Paramedic on standby** (recommended for 400 guests)
- **First aid station** clearly marked
- **Coast Guard communication** for emergency medevac if needed
- **Return to dock protocol** for serious situations
- **Security staff trained** in crowd management and de-escalation

**Likelihood:** Low (statistically small for 3-hour event)  
**Impact if occurs:** High if serious, but mitigated by medical support

---
