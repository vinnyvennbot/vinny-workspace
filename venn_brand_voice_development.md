# VENN BRAND VOICE & SOCIAL PERSONALITY - EXECUTION GUIDE

## CORE BRAND VOICE DEFINITION

### The Venn Voice: "Sophisticated Storyteller"

**Primary Characteristics:**
- **Curiously Informed:** We know SF's secrets but share them with wonder, not smugness
- **Intimately Sophisticated:** High-end without being pretentious, exclusive without being elitist  
- **Story-Driven:** Every venue has a narrative; every experience creates new chapters
- **Authentically Mysterious:** We reveal secrets gradually, building anticipation naturally

### Voice Personality Breakdown:

#### Tone Spectrum (1-10 scale):
```
Formal ←→ Conversational: 7/10 (Approachably sophisticated)
Serious ←→ Playful: 6/10 (Thoughtfully engaging) 
Traditional ←→ Modern: 8/10 (Contemporary with respect for history)
Reserved ←→ Bold: 7/10 (Confident but not flashy)
Practical ←→ Aspirational: 8/10 (Inspiring experiences within reach)
```

## MESSAGING FRAMEWORK

### Core Message Pillars:

#### Pillar 1: Discovery & Access
**What we say:** "We unlock San Francisco's most extraordinary hidden venues"
**How we say it:** With wonder and exclusivity balanced
**Avoid:** Making it sound impossible to access or overly precious

#### Pillar 2: Storytelling & History
**What we say:** "Every venue has a story worth experiencing" 
**How we say it:** As curious explorers sharing discoveries
**Avoid:** Academic lecturing or dry historical recitations

#### Pillar 3: Intimacy & Connection
**What we say:** "Small gatherings, big memories"
**How we say it:** Warm, personal, relationship-focused
**Avoid:** Exclusivity that feels unwelcoming or snobby

#### Pillar 4: Curation & Quality
**What we say:** "Every detail matters in creating extraordinary experiences"
**How we say it:** Confident expertise without pretension
**Avoid:** Perfectionism that feels unattainable or cold

### Language Guidelines:

#### Words We Love:
- Discover, uncover, reveal
- Hidden, secret, tucked away
- Intimate, curated, extraordinary
- Stories, memories, experiences
- Awakening, unlocking, opening
- Whispers, echoes, remembers (for historical elements)

#### Words We Avoid:
- Exclusive (use "intimate" instead)
- Elite (use "sophisticated" instead) 
- Luxury (use "extraordinary" instead)
- Best (use "remarkable" or "exceptional")
- Amazing/incredible (overused)
- Obviously, clearly, definitely (assumes too much)

#### Sentence Starters We Use:
- "What we found..."
- "Behind this door..."
- "For [X] years, this space..."
- "We've discovered..."
- "This [month], we're..."
- "Some stories..."
- "Hidden beneath..."

#### Calls-to-Action (CTAs):
**Soft CTAs:** "Join us," "Explore with us," "Discover more"
**Direct CTAs:** "Reserve your place," "Link in bio," "Limited seating"
**Curiosity CTAs:** "What story should we uncover next?" "Guess the venue"

## BRAND VOICE EVOLUTION

### Quarterly Voice Audits:
**Review Questions:**
- Are we maintaining sophistication without pretension?
- Do our stories feel authentic or manufactured? 
- Are we building community or just talking at followers?
- Is our mystery compelling or confusing?
- Are we staying true to SF's character while building our own?

### Voice Metrics to Track:
- Comment sentiment analysis
- Brand mention tone tracking
- Community engagement quality
- Story completion rates (proxy for compelling voice)
- Share-to-like ratios (measure of resonance)

### Team Voice Training:

#### Daily Voice Check:
**Before posting, ask:**
1. Does this sound like Venn discovered something wonderful?
2. Would I want to share this discovery with a close friend?
3. Does this honor both the venue's history and our brand personality?
4. Is this mysterious enough to intrigue but clear enough to understand?
5. Does this invite community without excluding anyone?

#### Voice Calibration Examples:
**Too Formal:** "We cordially invite distinguished guests to partake in an exceptional culinary journey."
**Just Right:** "We're opening doors that haven't welcomed guests in decades. Join us."
**Too Casual:** "Found this sick hidden spot! You guys are gonna love it!"

#### Voice Don'ts:
- Don't oversell the experience (let discovery speak for itself)
- Don't talk down to the audience (they're curious, not clueless)
- Don't reveal everything (preserve some mystery)
- Don't ignore SF's diverse voices (our stories should reflect the city's richness)
- Don't sound like every other premium dining brand

The Venn voice is distinctive: we're storytellers first, marketers second. Every piece of content should feel like sharing a fascinating discovery with someone we respect and want to delight.

**Voice Mantra:** "Sophisticated curiosity with a story worth sharing."
