## 📊 PRICING TIERS (Reference Guide)

### Tier 1: Presenting Sponsor ($10,000-25,000)
**Best For:** Large events (250+ guests), corporate buyouts, signature experiences
**Includes:**
- Exclusive category sponsor (no competing brands)
- "Presented by [BRAND]" naming rights
- 10-20 VIP tickets
- Full brand integration (signage, product, content)
- Pre/during/post-event marketing campaign

**Target Brands:** Major tech companies (Salesforce, Stripe), premium alcohol brands (Hendrick's, Moët), financial services

---

### Tier 2: Premier Partner ($5,000-10,000)
**Best For:** Mid-size events (100-200 guests), strong brand alignment, co-marketing opportunities
**Includes:**
- Category sponsor (1-2 brands per category)
- Logo on all marketing materials
- 4-8 VIP tickets
- Product sampling / activation booth
- Social media campaign (3-5 posts)

**Target Brands:** Craft distilleries (Distillery 209, St. George Spirits), lifestyle brands (Williams Sonoma), local companies

---

### Tier 3: Supporting Sponsor ($2,000-5,000)
**Best For:** Smaller events (50-100 guests), emerging brands, gift bag sponsors
**Includes:**
- Logo on event page + signage
- 2-4 complimentary tickets
- Product placement (gift bags, table displays)
- Social media mention (1-2 posts)

**Target Brands:** Wine brands, fashion boutiques, local restaurants, cultural organizations

---

### Tier 4: In-Kind Partner ($0 cash + $1,000-3,000 product value)
**Best For:** Beverage/food suppliers, equipment rentals, service providers
**Includes:**
- Product/service donation (e.g., wine for cocktails, floral arrangements)
- Logo on event page
- 1-2 complimentary tickets
- Social media thank you post

**Target Brands:** Wineries, bakeries, equipment rental companies, transportation services

## ✅ PRE-PITCH RESEARCH CHECKLIST

Before sending deck to any brand, answer these questions:

**Brand Research:**
- [ ] What is their current marketing focus? (check recent Instagram, press releases)
- [ ] Who is their target demographic? (does it match our audience?)
- [ ] What events/partnerships have they done recently? (similar or different from ours?)
- [ ] What are their brand values? (sustainability, craftsmanship, innovation, etc.)
- [ ] Budget range? (large corp = $10-25K possible, small brand = $2-5K realistic)

**Alignment Check:**
- [ ] Product fit: Does their product naturally integrate into our event? (not forced)
- [ ] Aesthetic match: Does their brand vibe align with our event vibe?
- [ ] Audience overlap: Do they want to reach our attendees?
- [ ] Timing: Do they have budget available now? (Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 planning cycles)

**Competition:**
- [ ] Are they already sponsoring competing SF events?
- [ ] Do they have exclusive partnerships that would conflict?
- [ ] Are there multiple brands we could approach in same category? (prioritize which first)

## 🎯 SUCCESS METRICS TO TRACK (Post-Event Reporting)

**Quantitative Metrics:**
- **Attendance:** [# guests] attended (out of [# tickets sold])
- **Social Reach:** [# Instagram posts] from attendees, [# impressions] estimated
- **Brand Mentions:** [# times] brand mentioned in social posts/stories
- **Product Interaction:** [# guests] sampled product / interacted with activation
- **Email Open Rate:** [%] opened post-event email featuring sponsor
- **Survey Results:** [%] of attendees recalled sponsor brand

**Qualitative Metrics:**
- **Guest Feedback:** "What did you think of [BRAND]'s presence?" (survey question)
- **Photo Quality:** Professional event photography showing brand integration
- **Content Created:** [# blog posts / Instagram reels / TikToks] featuring sponsor
- **Press Mentions:** Did any media coverage mention the sponsor?

**Deliverables to Sponsor:**
- **Week 1 post-event:** Photo gallery (50-100 high-res images featuring brand)
- **Week 2 post-event:** Performance report (PDF with all metrics above)
- **Ongoing:** Any press mentions, social posts that mention brand (send as they happen)

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Lead with value, not ask:** Frame as "opportunity to reach [TARGET]" not "we need money"
2. **Show, don't tell:** Mockups of brand integration > vague promises
3. **Be specific:** "$5,000 for 100 in-person impressions" > "great brand exposure"
4. **Customize every pitch:** Generic decks get ignored, specific alignment gets responses
5. **Start high, negotiate down:** Anchor at $10K, settle at $7K (vs. start at $5K, get $3K)
6. **Offer tiers:** Give them choice ("Premier $8K or Supporting $4K") not take-it-or-leave-it
7. **Create urgency:** "We're finalizing sponsors by [DATE]" > "let us know whenever"
8. **Follow up persistently:** 3-5 follow-ups is normal for B2B sales, not annoying
9. **Reference past success:** Once you have 1 sponsor, use as case study for next
10. **Think long-term:** Offer series discount ("Sponsor 4 events this year for $15K vs. $5K each")
