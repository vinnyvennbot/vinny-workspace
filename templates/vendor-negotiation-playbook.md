# Vendor Negotiation Playbook
**Purpose:** Strategic framework for negotiating with vendors to achieve best pricing while maintaining relationships

---

## Core Principles

### The Venn Approach
1. **Respectful Always:** We're building long-term relationships, not burning bridges
2. **Transparent About Scale:** Share our growth vision (2 events/month target)
3. **Competitive Leverage:** Use quotes from multiple vendors to negotiate
4. **Win-Win Mindset:** Find creative ways to reduce costs without hurting their business

### What We NEVER Do
- ❌ Lie about competing quotes
- ❌ Ghost vendors after getting quotes
- ❌ Mention budget constraints in first outreach
- ❌ Pressure vendors with fake urgency
- ❌ Badmouth competitors to get lower prices

### What We ALWAYS Do
- ✅ Acknowledge quotes promptly
- ✅ Be honest about timeline and decision process
- ✅ Offer repeat business potential
- ✅ Provide referrals/testimonials when vendors deliver
- ✅ Pay invoices on time

## Negotiation Stages

## Stage 1: Initial Outreach (No Negotiation)

**Goal:** Get baseline quotes from 20+ vendors per category

**Email Template Elements:**
- Event details (date, time, guest count, theme)
- Request for availability and pricing
- Professional but warm tone
- **NEVER mention budget range**

**Why No Budget Discussion:**
- Vendors will anchor to top of your range
- You lose negotiating leverage
- May miss vendors who would come in lower
- First quotes are often inflated (room to negotiate)

**Example:**
```
We're organizing a Western-themed event on March 15 with 175 guests.
Could you share your availability and pricing for [service]?
```

**NOT:**
```
We have a budget of $2,000 for [service]. Can you work with that?
```

---

## Stage 2: Quote Collection & Analysis

**After Receiving Quotes:**

1. **Log Everything** (Mission Control database)
   - Vendor name, contact, quote amount, what's included
   - Response time (faster = more interested)
   - Professionalism of response

2. **Create Comparison Matrix** (Google Sheet)
   ```
   Vendor Name | Quote | What's Included | Notes | Rating
   Vendor A    | $2,500| Setup, 4hrs, breakdown | Fast response | 4/5
   Vendor B    | $1,800| 4hrs only, we setup | Bare bones | 3/5
   Vendor C    | $2,200| Full service + staff | Best value | 5/5
   ```

3. **Identify Negotiation Targets**
   - **Top tier:** Best service, high price (negotiate down)
   - **Mid tier:** Good service, fair price (confirm and book)
   - **Bottom tier:** Low price but red flags (use as leverage only)

## Stage 3: Negotiation Tactics

### Tactic 1: Volume Discount (Best for Repeat Services)

**When to Use:** DJ, catering, photography (services we'll need monthly)

**Email Template:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

Thank you for the quote of $[AMOUNT]. We're impressed with your work.

Quick question: Venn Social is planning 2 events per month going forward. 
If we commit to booking you for multiple events this quarter, is there 
flexibility on the per-event pricing?

We're looking to build long-term vendor partnerships rather than one-off bookings.

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**Expected Result:** 10-20% discount for commitment to 3+ events

---

### Tactic 2: Competitive Comparison (Use Carefully)

**When to Use:** When you have multiple similar quotes and want best pricing

**Email Template:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

Thank you for the quote of $[AMOUNT]. We're reviewing proposals from 
several vendors and your [SERVICE/QUALITY] stands out.

We've received quotes ranging from $[LOW] to $[HIGH]. Is there any 
flexibility in your pricing to help us make this work?

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**Key Rules:**
- ✅ Use real quote ranges (don't fabricate)
- ✅ Compliment their service first (show you value them)
- ✅ Give them room to say no (no pressure)
- ❌ Don't name competing vendors
- ❌ Don't pit vendors against each other aggressively

**Expected Result:** 5-15% price reduction or added value

---

### Tactic 3: Value-Add Negotiation (Get More, Pay Same)

**When to Use:** Price is fair but you want more for your money

**Email Template:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

The $[AMOUNT] quote works for our budget. Before we move forward, 
I wanted to ask: is there any flexibility to extend the service time 
from [X] hours to [X+1] hours, or include [ADDITIONAL SERVICE]?

We want to make sure we're getting maximum value and that your team 
has enough time to deliver your best work.

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**Examples of Value-Adds:**
- DJ: Extra hour, enhanced lighting package, MC services
- Catering: Additional appetizers, upgraded ingredients, extra staff
- Photography: More hours, second shooter, faster turnaround
- Venue: Earlier access, included AV, discounted bar minimums

**Expected Result:** 10-30% more value without price increase

---

### Tactic 4: Payment Terms Flexibility

**When to Use:** Price is firm but cash flow is concern

**Email Template:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

We'd like to move forward with your quote of $[AMOUNT]. 

For our accounting process: is it possible to split the payment 
into a deposit now and balance 7 days after the event? Or would 
you prefer [ALTERNATIVE PAYMENT SCHEDULE]?

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**Why This Works:**
- Shows commitment (you're booking)
- Helps cash flow (event revenue comes before final payment)
- Most vendors are flexible on timing once you've committed

---

### Tactic 5: Partnership/Exposure Trade (Use Sparingly)

**When to Use:** Vendors who value marketing exposure, smaller/newer businesses

**Email Template:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

Your quote of $[AMOUNT] is a bit above our budget. Here's a creative idea:

What if we feature you prominently in our marketing (Instagram posts, 
event page, attendee emails) and provide testimonials/photos post-event? 
We typically reach [X] people per event across social media.

Would that justify a reduced rate of $[LOWER AMOUNT]?

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**When This Works:**
- Newer vendors building portfolio
- Vendors with strong social media presence (value exposure)
- Unique/specialty services (want to showcase work)

**When This DOESN'T Work:**
- Established vendors (they don't need exposure)
- Corporate vendors (exposure isn't currency)

**Expected Result:** 10-25% discount in exchange for marketing

## Stage 4: Closing the Deal

### Confirming the Booking

**Once Price is Agreed:**

1. **Confirm in Writing (Email)**
```
Hi [Vendor],

Excited to move forward! To confirm:

- Event: [EVENT NAME]
- Date: [DAY OF WEEK, MONTH DATE, YEAR]
- Service: [WHAT THEY'RE PROVIDING]
- Price: $[AMOUNT]
- Payment: [DEPOSIT AMOUNT] deposit, [BALANCE] due [DATE]

Please send over the contract when ready. Looking forward to working together!

Best regards,
Vinny
```

2. **Request Contract**
   - Never confirm without signed contract
   - Review contract for cancellation terms, liability, payment schedule
   - Flag any concerns to Zed before signing

3. **Update Mission Control Database**
   - Mark vendor status: 'contract_sent' → 'booked'
   - Log deposit amount and due date
   - Set reminder for balance payment

## Negotiation Red Flags

**When to Walk Away:**
- Vendor refuses to provide written quote (everything verbal)
- Pricing changes significantly between verbal and written quote
- Contract has unreasonable cancellation penalties (>50% deposit forfeit)
- Vendor is defensive or rude during negotiation
- Gut feeling says something is off

**When to Pay Full Price:**
- Vendor is highly recommended (referrals from trusted sources)
- Quality is critical and they're known as best in category
- Time is tight and alternatives are risky
- Price is already fair/competitive (not everything needs negotiation)

## Post-Event Relationship Building

### After Successful Event

**Always Send Thank You Email:**
```
Hi [Vendor],

Just wanted to say thank you for your work at [EVENT NAME] on [DATE]. 
[SPECIFIC COMPLIMENT: The food was incredible / The photos captured the 
energy perfectly / etc.]

We received great feedback from attendees and would love to work with 
you again on future events. I'll keep you in mind as we plan our next one!

Best regards,
Vinny
```

**Update RELATIONSHIPS.md:**
- Performance rating (1-5 scale)
- What went well
- What could improve
- Likelihood to rebook
- Any negotiation notes for next time

### Building Long-Term Partnerships

**For Vendors We Want to Use Repeatedly:**

1. **Quarterly Check-Ins** (even when not booking)
   - "Hey, just wanted to stay in touch. How's business?"
   - Keeps relationship warm, shows we care beyond transactions

2. **Referrals When Possible**
   - If someone asks for vendor recs, recommend our partners
   - Tag them in Instagram posts, give credit publicly

3. **Early Access to Upcoming Events**
   - "We're planning [EVENT] for [DATE]. Want to lock in your services 
     before we reach out to others?"
   - Makes them feel valued, often gets better pricing

## Negotiation Scripts by Vendor Type

### Catering

**Initial Ask:**
"What's your per-person pricing for [MENU STYLE] for 175 guests?"

**Negotiation Angle:**
- Volume: "We'll need catering 2x/month - any discount for regular bookings?"
- Flexibility: "If we simplify the menu or do buffet instead of plated, how does pricing change?"
- Value-add: "Can you include setup/breakdown and serving staff in that price?"

### DJ/Entertainment

**Initial Ask:**
"What's your rate for a 4-hour event on [DATE]?"

**Negotiation Angle:**
- Volume: "We have 2 events per month - interested in being our regular DJ?"
- Value-add: "Can you include uplighting or MC services at that rate?"
- Flexibility: "If we book you for 3 hours instead of 4, what's the pricing?"

### Venue

**Initial Ask:**
"What's your rate for [DATE] from [START] to [END] for 175 guests?"

**Negotiation Angle:**
- Day of week: "If we did Friday instead of Saturday, any discount?"
- Off-peak: "What if we did an afternoon event vs evening?"
- Repeat: "We're looking to host monthly events - any partnership rates?"

### Photography/Videography

**Initial Ask:**
"What's your package for [HOURS] of coverage on [DATE]?"

**Negotiation Angle:**
- Volume: "We need photographers for 2 events/month - any multi-event discount?"
- Value-add: "Can you include a second shooter or expedited turnaround?"
- Flexibility: "What if we reduced hours from 4 to 3?"

## Success Metrics

**Track These Per Vendor Category:**

1. **Average Discount Achieved:** Target 10-15% off initial quotes
2. **Repeat Booking Rate:** Target 50%+ vendors we want to work with again
3. **Payment Terms:** Target 50% deposit, 50% post-event for cash flow
4. **Response Time:** Target <48 hours from quote request to received quote
5. **Value-Adds Secured:** Extra hours, additional services, better terms

**Document in:**
- Mission Control database (per vendor)
- RELATIONSHIPS.md (performance notes)
- Memory files (lessons learned)

---

## Remember

**Negotiation is about partnership, not combat.**

The best vendors become long-term partners who:
- Give us priority scheduling
- Offer better rates because they know us
- Go above and beyond because we treat them well
- Refer us to other quality vendors

Treat every negotiation as the start of a relationship, not a one-time transaction.
