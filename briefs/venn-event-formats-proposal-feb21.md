## 🎭 FORMAT 1: Speakeasy Time Capsule Series
**Theme:** Rotating decade experiences in hidden SF venues

### Concept
Monthly speakeasy-style events that transport attendees to different eras (1920s, 1950s, 1980s, 2000s). Each month = different decade, different hidden venue, period-appropriate entertainment and dress code encouraged.

### Target Audience
- 25-40 professionals
- History/nostalgia enthusiasts  
- Social club seekers
- 100-150 guests per event

### Venue Strategy
- **Rotation model**: Presidio Officers' Club (1920s), The Chapel (1950s), DNA Lounge (1980s), Rickhouse (2000s)
- **Advantages**: Existing venue relationships, built-in mystique, each location fits era aesthetic
- **Cost**: $2,500-4,000/venue rental (existing relationships reduce negotiation time)

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $50 early bird, $65 standard, $85 VIP (period cocktails + reserved seating)
- **Capacity**: 120 guests average
- **Revenue**: $7,800 (EB) + breakeven at ~60 tickets
- **Sponsorship**: Period brand tie-ins (vintage clothing, classic car dealerships, record shops)

### Cost Structure (120 guests)
- Venue: $3,500
- Entertainment (live band/DJ): $1,500
- Period decorations/props: $800
- Bartending/cocktails: $2,000
- Photography: $600
- Marketing: $400
- **Total**: $8,800 | **Per Person**: $73 | **Profit Margin**: 15-25% at $50-65 tickets

### Differentiation
- **vs. SF Social Club**: More thematic depth, rotating venues create discovery element
- **vs. Generic bar events**: Story-driven immersion, period entertainment, curated experience
- **Venn Advantage**: Venue access, storytelling brand, community trust

### First Event Proposal: "Roaring 20s Speakeasy" at Presidio Officers' Club
- Date: April 12, 2026 (Saturday)
- Theme: Prohibition-era glamour
- Entertainment: Jazz trio + Charleston dance lesson
- Dress code: 1920s encouraged (prizes for best dressed)
- Unique hook: "Password at the door" speakeasy entrance

## 🌃 FORMAT 2: SF Neighborhood Night Market Series
**Theme:** Celebrate SF's diverse neighborhoods through food, music, and local vendors

### Concept
Quarterly rotating neighborhood celebrations featuring 15-20 local food vendors, live music from neighborhood artists, and curated experiences showcasing each district's unique character.

### Target Audience  
- 25-50 locals and tourists
- Foodies and culture enthusiasts
- Community builders
- 300-500 guests per event

### Neighborhood Rotation
1. **Mission District** (Spring): Latin food, muralist demo, salsa band
2. **Chinatown** (Summer): Dim sum vendors, dragon dance, Chinese classical music
3. **Haight-Ashbury** (Fall): Vintage vendors, 60s tribute band, tie-dye workshop
4. **North Beach** (Winter): Italian vendors, opera singers, bocce tournament

### Venue Strategy
- **Large open spaces**: Fort Mason Green, Presidio Main Parade Ground, Mission Bay Park
- **Permits required**: SF Parks & Rec (3-month lead time)
- **Cost**: $1,500-3,000 venue + $500-1,000 permits

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $35 early bird, $45 standard (includes $10 food credit)
- **Capacity**: 400 guests average
- **Revenue**: $18,000 (ticket sales) + $4,000 (vendor booth fees) = $22,000
- **Sponsorship**: Neighborhood business associations, SF Tourism, local breweries

### Cost Structure (400 guests)
- Venue + permits: $4,000
- 3 music acts: $3,000
- Vendor coordination: $1,500
- Marketing: $1,500
- Staff/security: $2,000
- Insurance: $800
- **Total**: $12,800 | **Per Person**: $32 | **Profit Margin**: 40-50%

### Differentiation
- **vs. Off the Grid**: Curated experience vs. open market, neighborhood storytelling
- **vs. Street fairs**: Evening timing (6-10 PM), adult-focused, premium curation
- **Venn Advantage**: Community relationships, storytelling, ticket model creates commitment

## 🎨 FORMAT 3: Art Mansion After Dark
**Theme:** Late-night art experiences in SF's historic mansions

### Concept
Exclusive after-hours access to SF's historic mansions with rotating art installations, live art creation, intimate performances, and cocktails. Partner with local artists for installations that respond to mansion history.

### Target Audience
- 28-45 art collectors and enthusiasts
- Design professionals
- Cultural sophisticates
- 80-100 guests per event (intimate)

### Venue Strategy
- **Target venues**: Haas-Lilienthal House, Flood Mansion, Burlingame estates
- **Partnership model**: Revenue share with historical societies (they get exposure + funding)
- **Cost**: $3,000-5,000 venue rental (offset by cultural mission alignment)

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $75 early bird, $95 standard, $125 VIP (artist meet & greet)
- **Capacity**: 90 guests average
- **Revenue**: $8,500
- **Art Sales Commission**: 20% of artist sales (estimated $2,000/event)
- **Total Revenue**: $10,500

### Cost Structure (90 guests)
- Venue: $4,000
- 3-5 featured artists (stipends): $2,500
- Catering/bar: $2,700
- Sound/lighting: $800
- Marketing: $500
- **Total**: $10,500 | **Per Person**: $117 | **Profit Margin**: Break-even to 15% (brand building)

### Differentiation
- **vs. Gallery openings**: Exclusive access to spaces not normally available, mansion storytelling
- **vs. Museum nights**: More intimate, artist interaction, cocktail social focus
- **Venn Advantage**: Unique venue access, community of cultural sophisticates

### Strategic Value
- **Higher-end audience** builds Venn brand prestige
- **Artist partnerships** create content and word-of-mouth
- **Scalable**: Can add VIP tiers, private tours, art auction nights

## 🍷 FORMAT 4: SF Winery Rooftop Tour
**Theme:** Hidden rooftop wineries and urban wine experiences

### Concept
Guided tour of SF's urban wineries with rooftop tastings, winemaker talks, food pairings, and sunset views. Partner with 3-4 urban wineries for exclusive access and education.

### Target Audience
- 28-50 wine enthusiasts
- Couples and friend groups
- Professional networkers
- 40-50 guests per tour (intimate)

### Venue/Partner Strategy
- **Partner wineries**: Bluxome Street Winery, SF Wine Collective, Westwood Wine Bar (rooftop)
- **Route**: 3 stops, 1.5 hours each, chartered bus transport
- **Cost**: Winery tasting fees ($20/person) + bus charter ($800)

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $95 early bird, $115 standard
- **Capacity**: 45 guests
- **Revenue**: $5,200
- **Sponsorship**: Wine distributors, cheese brands

### Cost Structure (45 guests)
- Winery fees: $900
- Bus charter: $800
- Food pairings: $900
- Tour guide/host: $300
- Marketing: $300
- **Total**: $3,200 | **Per Person**: $71 | **Profit Margin**: 38-45%

### Differentiation
- **vs. Wine tours**: Urban focus, rooftop exclusive access vs. Napa bus tour
- **vs. Wine bars**: Educational + social, multi-venue discovery
- **Venn Advantage**: Curated partners, storytelling about urban winemaking

### Scalability
- Saturday afternoon tours (2-6 PM)
- Can run 2x/month with different routes
- Seasonal variations (harvest tour, barrel tasting, blending workshop)

## 🎵 FORMAT 5: Secret Supper Club Concert Series
**Theme:** Intimate 20-person dinners with live performance by emerging artists

### Concept
Ultra-intimate dinner experiences (20 guests max) in unique private spaces with live acoustic performance by SF emerging musicians. Family-style meal, artist stories between songs, meet & greet.

### Target Audience
- 30-55 music lovers and foodies
- Couples seeking unique date nights
- Community builders
- 20 guests (intentionally small)

### Venue Strategy
- **Private dining rooms**: The Barrel Room, Presidio Officer's Club, private lofts
- **Artist partnerships**: Revenue share with musicians (exposure + guaranteed payment)
- **Cost**: $800-1,500 venue

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $125 per person (all-inclusive)
- **Capacity**: 20 guests
- **Revenue**: $2,500
- **Artist partnership**: $500 guaranteed + tips

### Cost Structure (20 guests)
- Venue: $1,200
- Catering (family-style): $800
- Artist fee: $500
- Wine/cocktails: $400
- Marketing: $200
- **Total**: $3,100 | **Per Person**: $155 | **Profit Margin**: -20% (loss leader for community)

### Strategic Value
- **Community building**: Strongest intimacy creates super-fans
- **Artist pipeline**: Builds relationships with rising musicians (future partnerships)
- **Premium positioning**: Justifies higher ticket prices for other events
- **Content gold**: Intimate moments = powerful social proof

### Differentiation
- **vs. Concerts**: Intimacy, dinner included, artist interaction
- **vs. Supper clubs**: Live music focus, storytelling, emerging artist discovery
- **Venn Advantage**: Community trust enables $125 tickets for unknown artists

## 🏃 FORMAT 6: Urban Adventure Scavenger Hunt
**Theme:** Team-based challenges exploring hidden SF gems

### Concept
3-hour competitive scavenger hunt through SF neighborhoods with challenges at local businesses, historical sites, and hidden gems. Teams of 4-6 earn points through photo/video challenges, trivia, and creative tasks.

### Target Audience
- 24-40 professionals
- Team building groups
- Competitive friend groups
- 60-80 guests (10-15 teams)

### Route Strategy
- **Rotating neighborhoods**: Financial District secrets, Mission murals, Presidio history
- **Partner businesses**: Challenges at partner locations (free marketing for them)
- **Cost**: Route planning + challenge design ($500 one-time, reusable)

### Revenue Model
- **Ticket Price**: $45 per person (teams of 4-6)
- **Capacity**: 72 guests (12 teams)
- **Revenue**: $3,240
- **Sponsorship**: Athletic brands, local businesses on route

### Cost Structure (72 guests)
- Route design/challenges: $500 (amortized over 4 events = $125)
- Prizes (winning team): $400
- Event host/referee: $300
- Marketing: $300
- Supplies (challenge materials): $200
- **Total**: $1,325 | **Per Person**: $18 | **Profit Margin**: 60%+

### Differentiation
- **vs. Bar crawls**: Daytime/afternoon, challenge-based, local discovery
- **vs. Corporate team building**: Accessible pricing, open to public, fun over training
- **Venn Advantage**: SF knowledge, local business relationships, community

### Scalability
- **High margin + low cost** = can run weekly
- **Seasonal variations**: Holiday hunts, themed routes
- **Corporate upsell**: Private team building ($2,000 flat fee for 30 people)
