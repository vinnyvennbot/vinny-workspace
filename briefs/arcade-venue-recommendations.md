# Arcade Venue Recommendations for Nostalgia Event

## Top Recommendation: Joey the Cat Mission Arcade ⭐
**Address**: 3252 19th St, San Francisco, CA 94110  
**Website**: https://www.joeythecat.com/mission-arcade  
**Capacity**: 150  
**Type**: Fully private venue (no public hours)

### Why Joey the Cat is Perfect:
- **Fully Private**: Exclusive buyout, zero public overlap
- **Complete Package**: Bar + food + games included (one-stop shop)
- **20+ Arcade Games**: Skee-Ball (tournament-grade), Pac-Man, Air Hockey, Basketball, Boxer, photobooth
- **Catering Included**: Pizza (Outta Sight Pizza), Taco Bar, Arcade Snacks
- **Staffed Full Bar**: Craft cocktails, wine, local/craft beers
- **AV Setup**: 3x 100" TVs (streaming + HDMI), house audio + mics
- **Easy Load-In**: Roll-up door access
- **Location**: Mission District, walkable from 16th St BART

### Pricing Options (from their site):
- **Unlimited Arcade**: $15/person/hour
- **Prepaid Game Cards**: $10 or $20 per person (refillable)
- **Food Packages**: Pizza buffet, taco bar, unlimited pizza options
- **Booking**: 3-4 weeks advance recommended

### Perfect For:
- Nostalgia-themed events (retro games)
- Corporate team building
- Private parties 50-150 guests
- Brand activations

## Alternative Option 2: Emporium Arcade Bar
**Address**: Harding Theater, San Francisco  
**Website**: https://www.emporiumarcadebar.com/locations/san-francisco/  
**Capacity**: ~200

### Pros:
- Historic 100-year-old Harding Theater venue
- Established arcade bar chain (Chicago-based)
- Games + drinks + music

### Cons:
- Likely shared space (not fully private)
- Less customization vs. Joey the Cat

## Recommendation:
**Lead with Joey the Cat** - it's purpose-built for private events, has the most complete package, and aligns perfectly with a nostalgia theme. Emporium is a solid backup if Joey the Cat is unavailable or over budget.
