## Executive Summary

Two speakeasy concepts researched with different scales and profitability profiles:

1. **Prohibition Speakeasy Night** — Larger scale (150-200 guests), profitable
2. **Midnight Secrets** — Intimate scale (60-80 guests), requires heavy sponsorship

**⚠️ CRITICAL FINDING:** The intimate version (Midnight Secrets) is **not financially viable** without $3,500-7,400 in sponsorships. Costs of $154-252/person vs. $95 ticket price = major loss.

---

## Option A: Prohibition Speakeasy Night (RECOMMENDED)

### Concept
1920s speakeasy with password entry, vintage cocktails, live jazz, and flapper fashion. Historic SF venue, 150-200 guests, $70-90/person.

### Financial Model (175 guests @ $80/ticket = $14,000 revenue)
- Venue: $3,000-5,000
- Jazz band: $1,500-2,500
- Bar/cocktails (2 included): $2,500
- Decorations: $800
- Photography: $600
- Marketing: $400
- Permits: $500
- **Total Cost:** $9,300-12,300
- **Profit:** $1,700-4,700 (12-33% margin)

### Profit Optimization
- VIP "Bootlegger" tier: $120 (reserved table + 4 drinks + photo)
- Vintage photo upsell: $20
- Merchandise: $15 speakeasy glasses
- **Potential Revenue:** $16,000-18,000
- **Potential Profit:** $4,000-6,000

### Top Venue Picks
1. **Bimbo's 365 Club** — Opened 1931, authentic history
2. **Foreign Cinema** — Hidden courtyard, vintage aesthetic
3. **Palace Hotel Garden Court** — Historic ballroom

### Pros
✅ Proven profitable model (12-33% margin)
✅ Larger capacity = more revenue
✅ Clear path to $4,000-6,000 profit with upsells
✅ Lower per-person costs due to scale

### Cons
❌ Larger venue harder to theme authentically
❌ Less intimate/exclusive feeling
❌ More guests = more complex logistics

## Option B: Midnight Secrets (⚠️ LOSS-MAKING)

### Concept
Ultra-intimate speakeasy (60-80 guests) with secret password/location reveal, live jazz trio, premium cocktails, vintage photography. Higher-end experience.

### Financial Model — THE PROBLEM
**ALL scenarios lose money without major sponsorships:**

#### Small Venue (60 guests @ $95/ticket = $5,700 revenue)
- Costs: $204/person ($12,240 total)
- Sponsorship needed: $3,500+ to break even
- **Loss:** -$59/person = -$3,540 total

#### Mid Venue (70 guests @ $95/ticket = $6,650 revenue)
- Costs: $251/person ($17,570 total)
- Sponsorship needed: $7,400+ to break even
- **Loss:** -$106/person = -$7,420 total

#### Premium Venue (80 guests @ $95/ticket = $7,600 revenue)
- Costs: $312/person ($24,960 total)
- Sponsorship needed: $12,000+ to break even
- **Loss:** -$157/person = -$12,560 total

### Why Costs Are So High
- Small capacity = venue/entertainment costs don't scale
- Premium speakeasy venues (Bourbon & Branch, Palace Theater) charge $4,000-12,000 rental
- Food/beverage minimums at high-end venues: $40-60/person
- Live jazz + photography + production = $3,000-4,500 fixed costs

### Top Venue Picks (All Expensive)
1. **The Speakeasy at Palace Theater** — $8,000-15,000 buyout
2. **Bourbon & Branch Private Rooms** — $5,000-10,000 + F&B minimums
3. **The Hideout** — $2,500-5,000 (smallest/cheapest option)

### Pros
✅ Ultra-exclusive, memorable experience
✅ Strong Instagram/word-of-mouth potential
✅ Premium positioning ($95 ticket justified)

### Cons
❌ **LOSES $3,500-12,500 without heavy sponsorships**
❌ Small capacity limits revenue ceiling
❌ High venue costs don't scale
❌ Requires securing $7,000+ in spirit brand partnerships to break even

## Recommendation

**APPROVE:** Prohibition Speakeasy Night (Option A)  
**PASS:** Midnight Secrets (Option B) — not viable without major sponsorship pipeline

### Why Prohibition Night Wins
1. **Profitable without sponsorships** ($1,700-6,000 profit)
2. **Larger scale** = more revenue ceiling
3. **Lower risk** — proven economics
4. **Sponsorships = bonus**, not requirement
5. **Easier to execute** — more venue options, simpler logistics

### Why Midnight Secrets Fails
1. **Guaranteed loss** without $3,500-12,000 in sponsorships
2. **High-risk dependency** on securing spirit brand partners
3. **Small capacity** limits revenue recovery
4. **Premium venues too expensive** for intimate scale
5. **Better as future event** once Venn has strong sponsor relationships
