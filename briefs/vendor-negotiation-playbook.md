## Core Principles

### 1. **Never Mention Budget in First Contact**
**Why:** Budget anchors negotiation at our ceiling, not vendor's floor.

**Template Approach:**
```
❌ WRONG: "We have $5,000 for a DJ"
✅ RIGHT: "What are your rates for a 4-hour event on March 15th?"
```

**After Quote Received:**
- If over budget: "We're working with a different budget range. Is there flexibility?"
- If way over: "That's outside our range. We're targeting closer to $X. Is that workable?"
- If under budget: Accept gracefully, don't negotiate down further (build goodwill)

### 2. **Competitive Leveraging Without Naming Names**
**Effective Phrases:**
- "We're reviewing several proposals in this range..."
- "Other vendors have come in around $X for similar services..."
- "We're comparing options to find the best fit..."

**Never:**
- "Vendor X quoted $Y" (unprofessional, burns bridges)
- "Can you beat this price?" (sounds cheap, not strategic)

### 3. **Build Relationships, Not Transactions**
**Long-term Value > Single Event Savings**

**Phrases That Build Goodwill:**
- "We run 2 events/month - hoping to find reliable partners..."
- "If this goes well, we'd love to work together regularly..."
- "We're building a preferred vendor network..."

**Why It Works:**
- Vendors discount for repeat business potential
- Future bookings are easier (skip RFP process)
- They prioritize your events when busy

## Negotiation Tactics by Category

### Venues

**High-Leverage Points:**
1. **Date flexibility** - "We could do Saturday OR Sunday..."
2. **Off-season timing** - "January is slower for you, right?"
3. **Weekday vs. Weekend** - "How much less is Thursday?"
4. **Multi-event commitment** - "Book 3 dates, get better rate?"

**Low-Leverage Points:**
1. Peak season weekends (Friday/Saturday in summer)
2. Last-minute bookings (<30 days)
3. High-demand dates (holidays, festivals)

**Real Example - Presidio Golf:**
- Katie quoted $10K for April 19, $14K for March Sundays
- **Lesson:** Date selection = biggest pricing lever (40% difference)
- **Action:** Always ask "What dates are cheaper?"

### Entertainment (DJ, Bands, Performers)

**Package Optimization:**
- "Do you offer shorter sets?" (3 hours vs. 4 hours)
- "What if we provide our own sound system?"
- "Can we skip the lighting package?"

**Timing Discounts:**
- Afternoon events often cheaper than evening
- Sunday rates sometimes lower than Saturday
- Weekday corporate rates may apply to weekend socials

**Real Example - All Ears DJ:**
- $1,400 quoted for 4 hours
- **Potential negotiation:** "What's your rate for 3 hours?" (might save $200-300)
- **Relationship play:** "We book DJs monthly - interested in preferred partner rate?"

### Catering

**Per-Person Pricing Traps:**
- Always ask for exact headcount ranges
- "Is the $50/person rate firm for 100-150 guests?"
- Caterers often have tiers (1-50, 51-100, 101-200)

**Service Level Adjustments:**
- Buffet vs. plated service (buffet = 30-40% cheaper)
- Appetizer-only vs. full meal
- Self-serve bar vs. bartended
- Disposable vs. real plates

**Real Example - Catering Quotes:**
- Full buffet: $40-60/person
- Appetizer buffet: $20-30/person
- **Lesson:** Menu selection = biggest cost variable

### Rentals (Equipment, Decor, Special Items)

**Multi-Item Bundling:**
- "If we rent tables, chairs, AND linens, what's the package price?"
- "Can you throw in setup/teardown for free at that volume?"

**Duration Discounts:**
- "Do you charge by day or by event?" (overnight = 2 days or 1?)
- "Is weekend rate different from weekday?"

**Real Example - Mechanical Bull:**
- Astrojump: $1,299 (includes attendant, 3 hours)
- Party Jump: Similar range
- **Negotiation angle:** "What's the rate for 4 hours instead of 3?"

## Timing Strategy

### When to Negotiate

**✅ GOOD TIMES:**
1. **After receiving quote** - vendor expects back-and-forth
2. **When you have alternatives** - leverage other quotes
3. **Multi-event commitment** - "Book 3, get discount?"
4. **Off-season/slow periods** - vendor needs to fill calendar

**❌ BAD TIMES:**
1. **Before quote** - no anchor to negotiate from
2. **Peak season/high demand** - vendor has other clients
3. **Last minute (<2 weeks)** - premium pricing for short notice
4. **After verbal agreement** - unprofessional to renegotiate

### Follow-Up Timing

**24-Hour Rule:**
- If no response within 24 hours → send polite follow-up
- "Just checking in - did you receive my inquiry about [date]?"

**Don't Ghost Vendors:**
- Even if declining, send acknowledgment
- "Thank you for the quote. We're going another direction, but appreciate your time."
- **Why:** Builds reputation, keeps door open for future

## Red Flags & Walk-Away Scenarios

### When to Walk Away

**🚩 Vendor Won't Budge + Way Over Budget:**
- If vendor is 50%+ over budget and shows zero flexibility
- **Action:** Thank them, move to alternatives

**🚩 Unprofessional Communication:**
- Slow responses (>48 hours consistently)
- Pushy/aggressive tactics
- Won't provide written quotes
- **Action:** Trust your gut, plenty of vendors in SF

**🚩 Hidden Fees:**
- "Oh, that doesn't include setup/teardown..."
- "Staffing is extra..."
- "Permits are your responsibility..."
- **Action:** Ask for ALL-IN pricing upfront

**🚩 No Contract/Sketchy Terms:**
- Wants cash only
- No cancellation policy
- Vague deliverables
- **Action:** Professional vendors have contracts, move on

### When to Pay Premium

**✅ WORTH PAYING MORE:**
1. **Proven reliability** - vendor worked with us before, delivered perfectly
2. **Unique capability** - only option for specific need
3. **High-stakes event** - flagship event, can't risk failure
4. **Time pressure** - last minute, premium justified

## Negotiation Workflow

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Initial Outreach** (No Budget Mention)
   ```
   - What: Service needed
   - When: Specific date
   - Where: Venue/location
   - Who: Guest count
   - How: Any special requirements
   ```

2. **Quote Received**
   ```
   - Log in Mission Control database immediately
   - Compare to budget target
   - Research alternatives (get 3+ quotes per category)
   ```

3. **Negotiation Decision Tree**
   ```
   Quote < Budget:
   → Accept or gentle negotiation for extras
   
   Quote = Budget ±10%:
   → Negotiate gently, focus on relationship building
   
   Quote > Budget 20-40%:
   → Request flexibility, mention competitive quotes
   
   Quote > Budget 50%+:
   → Thank + decline, move to alternatives
   ```

4. **Follow-Up**
   ```
   - Document all responses in database
   - Update RELATIONSHIPS.md with vendor reliability scores
   - Never leave vendors on read (acknowledge all quotes)
   ```

## Quick Reference: Negotiation Phrases

### Opening Negotiation
- "We're working within a budget of..."
- "Is there any flexibility on that number?"
- "What would be your best rate for..."
- "Other vendors are quoting in the $X range..."

### Seeking Discounts
- "Do you offer preferred partner rates for repeat clients?"
- "Is there a discount for booking multiple dates?"
- "What's the off-season/weekday rate?"
- "Can we reduce scope to hit our budget?"

### Relationship Building
- "We're planning to work with the same vendors regularly..."
- "Looking for long-term partnerships, not one-off transactions..."
- "If this goes well, we have 2 more events this quarter..."

### Graceful Decline
- "Thank you, but that's outside our budget range."
- "We're going another direction, but appreciate the quote."
- "We'll keep you in mind for future events when budget allows."
